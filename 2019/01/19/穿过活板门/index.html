Harry Potter and The Sorcerer’s Stone

Chapter Sixteen Through the Trapdoor

第十六章 穿过活板门

In years to come, Harry would never quite remember how he had managed to get through his exams when he half expected Voldemort to come bursting through the door at any moment. Yet the days crept by, and there could be no doubt that Fluffy was still alive and well behind the locked door.

在未来的岁月里，哈利将永远记不起他是如何通过考试的，当时他几乎预料伏地魔随时会破门而入。然而，日子一天天过去，毫无疑问，路威还活着，而且躲在锁着的门后面。

It was sweltering hot, especially in the large classroom where they did their written papers. They had been given special, new quills for the exams, which had been bewitched with an Anticheating spell.

天气闷热，尤其是在他们写论文的大教室里。他们在考试中被赋予了特殊的新羽毛笔，这些羽毛笔被施了防作弊咒。

They had practical exams as well. Professor Flitwick called them one by one into his class to see if they could make a pineapple tapdance across a desk. Professor McGonagall watched them turn a mouse into a snuffbox — points were given for how pretty the snuffbox was, but taken away if it had whiskers. Snape made them all nervous, breathing down their necks while they tried to remember how to make a Forgetfulness potion.

他们也有实践考试。弗立维教授把他们一个个叫到教室里，看他们能否在桌子上跳菠萝踢踏舞。麦格教授看着他们把一只老鼠变成了一个鼻烟盒——这个鼻烟盒有多漂亮就给分，但如果它有胡须就会被拿走。斯内普让他们都紧张起来，一边紧紧地盯着他们，一边试着回忆如何制作一种忘我药。

Harry did the best he could, trying to ignore the stabbing pains in his forehead, which had been bothering him ever since his trip into the forest. Neville thought Harry had a bad case of exam nerves because Harry couldn’t sleep, but the truth was that Harry kept being woken by his old nightmare, except that it was now worse than ever because there was a hooded figure dripping blood in it.

哈利尽了最大的努力，尽量不去理会额头上刺痛的疼痛，自从他走进森林以来，这种疼痛一直困扰着他。纳威认为哈利考试紧张得很厉害，因为哈利睡不着觉，但事实是，哈利一直被他过去的噩梦惊醒，只是现在比以前更糟了，因为有一个戴着兜帽的人在梦里往外滴血。

Maybe it was because they hadn’t seen what Harry had seen in the forest, or because they didn’t have scars burning on their foreheads, but Ron and Hermione didn’t seem as worried about the Stone as Harry. The idea of Voldemort certainly scared them, but he didn’t keep visiting them in dreams, and they were so busy with their studying they didn’t have much time to fret about what Snape or anyone else might be up to.

也许是因为他们没有看到哈利在森林里看到的东西，也许是因为他们额头上没有灼烧的伤疤，但罗恩和赫敏似乎不像哈利那样担心魔法石。伏地魔的想法当然吓坏了他们，但他并没有经常在梦中去看望他们，他们忙于学习，没有太多时间去担心斯内普或其他人可能会做什么。

Their very last exam was History of Magic. One hour of answering questions about batty old wizards who’d invented selfstirring cauldrons and they’d be free, free for a whole wonderful week until their exam results came out. When the ghost of Professor Binns told them to put down their quills and roll up their parchment, Harry couldn’t help cheering with the rest.

他们最后一次考试是魔法史。用一个小时回答关于发明了自动搅拌坩埚的古怪老巫师的问题，然后他们就可以自由了，自由了整整一周，直到考试结果出来。当宾斯教授的鬼魂叫他们放下羽毛笔，卷起羊皮纸时，哈利不禁和其他人一起欢呼起来。

“That was far easier than I thought it would be,” said Hermione as they joined the crowds flocking out onto the sunny grounds. “I needn’t have learned about the 1637 Werewolf Code of Conduct or the uprising of Elfric the Eager.”

“这比我想象的要容易得多。”赫敏说着，他们加入了蜂拥到阳光明媚的操场上的人群。“我不需要知道1637年狼人的行为准则或埃尔弗里克的起义。”

Hermione always liked to go through their exam papers afterward, but Ron said this made him feel ill, so they wandered down to the lake and flopped under a tree. The Weasley twins and Lee Jordan were tickling the tentacles of a giant squid, which was basking in the warm shallows. “No more studying,” Ron sighed happily, stretching out on the grass. “You could look more cheerful, Harry, we’ve got a week before we find out how badly we’ve done, there’s no need to worry yet.”

赫敏总是喜欢在考试结束后检查试卷，但罗恩说这让他觉得不舒服，于是他们就溜达到湖边，扑通一声倒在一棵树下。韦斯莱家的孪生兄弟和李·乔丹正在给一只正在温暖的浅滩里晒太阳的大乌贼的触须挠痒痒。“不用再学习了。”罗恩高兴地叹了口气，伸开四肢躺在草地上。“你可以高兴一点，哈利，我们还有一个星期才知道我们干得有多糟，现在还不用担心。”

Harry was rubbing his forehead.

哈利揉着额头。

“I wish I knew what this means!” he burst out angrily. “My scar keeps hurting — it’s happened before, but never as often as this.”

“我希望我知道这是什么意思!”他怒气冲冲地说。“我的伤疤一直在痛——以前有过，但从来没有像现在这样频繁。”

“Go to Madam Pomfrey,” Hermione suggested.

“去找庞弗雷夫人。”

“I’m not ill,” said Harry. “I think it’s a warning⋯ it means danger’s coming⋯”

“我没病，”哈利说。“我认为这是一个警告⋯这意味着危险来了⋯”

Ron couldn’t get worked up, it was too hot.

罗恩激动不起来，天太热了。

“Harry, relax, Hermione’s right, the Stone’s safe as long as Dumbledore’s around. Anyway, we’ve never had any proof Snape found out how to get past Fluffy. He nearly had his leg ripped off once, he’s not going to try it again in a hurry. And Neville will play Quidditch for England before Hagrid lets Dumbledore down.”

“哈利，别紧张，赫敏是对的，只要邓布利多在，魔法石就是安全的。”不管怎样，我们从来没有任何证据证明斯内普知道如何通过路威。有一次他的腿差点被割掉，他不打算再试一次。在海格让邓布利多失望之前，纳威将代表英格兰参加魁地奇比赛。

Harry nodded, but he couldn’t shake off a lurking feeling that there was something he’d forgotten to do, something important. When he tried to explain this, Hermione said, “That’s just the exams. I woke up last night and was halfway through my Transfiguration notes before I remembered we’d done that one.”

哈利点了点头，但他无法摆脱一种潜在的感觉，那就是他忘了做一件重要的事。当他试图解释时，赫敏说:“那只是考试。我昨晚醒来，正在复习变形课笔记，突然记起来我们已经做过了。”

Harry was quite sure the unsettled feeling didn’t have anything to do with work, though. He watched an owl flutter toward the school across the bright blue sky, a note clamped in its mouth. Hagrid was the only one who ever sent him letters. Hagrid would never betray Dumbledore. Hagrid would never tell anyone how to get past Fluffy⋯ never⋯ but⋯

哈利很确定这种不安的感觉与工作无关。他看见一只猫头鹰叼着一张纸条，在明亮的蓝天上向学校飞去。海格是唯一一个给他写信的人。海格永远不会背叛邓布利多。海格不会告诉任何人怎么走过去蓬松⋯永远⋯⋯

Harry suddenly jumped to his feet.

哈利突然跳了起来。

“Where’re you going?” said Ron sleepily.

“你要去哪里?罗恩睡意朦胧地说。

“I’ve just thought of something,” said Harry. He had turned white. “We’ve got to go and see Hagrid, now.”

“我刚想到一个办法，”哈利说。他脸色苍白。“我们现在得去看看海格。”

“Why?” panted Hermione, hurrying to keep up.

“为什么?赫敏喘着气说，急忙跟上。

“Don’t you think it’s a bit odd,” said Harry, scrambling up the grassy slope, “that what Hagrid wants more than anything else is a dragon, and a stranger turns up who just happens to have an egg in his pocket? How many people wander around with dragon eggs if it’s against wizard law? Lucky they found Hagrid, don’t you think? Why didn’t I see it before?”

“你不觉得有点奇怪吗，”哈利爬上草坡说，“海格最想要的是一条龙，而突然出现了一个陌生人，碰巧口袋里有个鸡蛋。”如果违反巫师法，有多少人会带着龙蛋四处游荡?幸运的是他们找到了海格，你不觉得吗?为什么我以前没见过?”

“What are you talking about?” said Ron, but Harry, sprinting across the grounds toward the forest, didn’t answer.

“你在说什么?”罗恩说，但哈利飞快地穿过草地，朝森林跑去，没有回答。

Hagrid was sitting in an armchair outside his house; his trousers and sleeves were rolled up, and he was shelling peas into a large bowl.

海格坐在屋外的一把扶手椅上;他的裤子和袖子都卷了起来，正在往一个大碗里剥豌豆皮。

“Hullo,” he said, smiling. “Finished yer exams? Got time fer a drink?”

“你好，”他微笑着说。“完成考试?有时间喝一杯吗?”

“Yes, please,” said Ron, but Harry cut him off.

“是的，求求你了。”罗恩说，但哈利打断了他的话。

“No, we’re in a hurry. Hagrid, I’ve got to ask you something. You know that night you won Norbert? What did the stranger you were playing cards with look like?”

“不，我们赶时间。”海格，我有件事要问你。你知道那天晚上你赢了诺伯特吗?和你一起玩牌的陌生人长什么样?”

“Dunno,” said Hagrid casually, “he wouldn’ take his cloak off.”

“不知道，”海格漫不经心地说，“他不肯脱掉斗篷。”

He saw the three of them look stunned and raised his eyebrows.

他看到他们三个人都惊呆了，扬起眉毛。

“It’s not that unusual, yeh get a lot o’ funny folk in the Hog’s Head — that’s the pub down in the village. Mighta bin a dragon dealer, mightn’ he? I never saw his face, he kept his hood up.”

“这并不奇怪，你在猪头酒吧里会遇到很多有趣的人——那是村里的酒吧。他可能是个卖龙的，是吗?我从没见过他的脸，他一直戴着兜帽。”

Harry sank down next to the bowl of peas. “What did you talk to him about, Hagrid? Did you mention Hogwarts at all?”

哈利在那碗豌豆旁边坐了下来。“你跟他说了些什么，海格?”你提到霍格沃茨了吗?”

“Mighta come up,” said Hagrid, frowning as he tried to remember. “Yeah⋯ he asked what I did, an’ I told him I was gamekeeper here⋯ He asked a bit about the sorta creatures I took after⋯ so I told him⋯ an’ I said what I’d always really wanted was a dragon⋯ an’ then⋯ I can’ remember too well, ‘cause he kept buyin’ me drinks⋯ Let’s see⋯ yeah, then he said he had the dragon egg an’ we could play cards fer it if I wanted⋯ but he had ter be sure I could handle it, he didn’ want it ter go ter any old home⋯ So I told him, after Fluffy, a dragon would be easy⋯”

“可能会上来的。”海格皱着眉头说，努力想记起来。“嗯⋯他问我所做的,”我告诉他,我在这里看守⋯他问一些关于近似生物后,我花了⋯⋯我告诉他“我总是说,我真正想要的是一个龙⋯⋯我可以记得太清楚,因为他一直buyin”我饮料⋯⋯是的,然后他说他的龙蛋”我们可以打牌带它如果我想⋯但他ter一定我可以处理它,他不希望它ter任何旧后回家⋯所以我告诉他,蓬松后,龙会容易⋯”

“And did he — did he seem interested in Fluffy?” Harry asked, trying to keep his voice calm.

“他——他好像对毛毛感兴趣吗?”哈利问道，尽量使自己的声音平静下来。

“Well — yeah — how many three-headed dogs d’yeh meet, even around Hogwarts? So I told him, Fluffy’s a piece o’ cake if yeh know how to calm him down, jus’ play him a bit o’ music an’ he’ll go straight off ter sleep—”

“嗯——是啊——你在霍格沃茨见过多少三头狗?”所以我告诉他，如果你知道怎么让他平静下来，给他放点音乐，他马上就会睡着的。”

Hagrid suddenly looked horrified.

海格突然看上去吓坏了。

“I shouldn’ta told yeh that!” he blurted out. “Forget I said it! Hey — where’re yeh goin’?”

“我不该告诉你的!”他脱口而出。“忘了我说过的话!”嘿——你去哪儿?”

Harry, Ron, and Hermione didn’t speak to each other at all until they came to a halt in the entrance hall, which seemed very cold and gloomy after the grounds.

哈利、罗恩和赫敏谁也没说话，直到他们在门厅里停了下来。

“We’ve got to go to Dumbledore,” said Harry. “Hagrid told that stranger how to get past Fluffy, and it was either Snape or Voldemort under that cloak — it must’ve been easy, once he’d got Hagrid drunk. I just hope Dumbledore believes us. Firenze might back us up if Bane doesn’t stop him. Where’s Dumbledore’s office?”

“我们得去找邓布利多。”海格告诉那个陌生人怎么从路威身边过去，那个披风下的不是斯内普就是伏地魔——一旦他把海格灌醉了，那一定很容易。我只希望邓布利多相信我们。如果贝恩不阻止他，费伦泽可能会支持我们。邓布利多的办公室在哪里?”

They looked around, as if hoping to see a sign pointing them in the right direction. They had never been told where Dumbledore lived, nor did they know anyone who had been sent to see him.

他们向四周看了看，好像希望能看到一个标志给他们指出正确的方向。他们从来没有听说过邓布利多住在哪里，也不知道有谁被派去见过他。

“We’ll just have to — ” Harry began, but a voice suddenly rang across the hall.

“我们不得不——”哈利开始说，但一个声音突然从大厅的另一头传来。

“What are you three doing inside?”

“你们三个在里面干什么?”

It was Professor McGonagall, carrying a large pile of books.

是麦格教授，手里拿着一大堆书。

“We want to see Professor Dumbledore,” said Hermione, rather bravely, Harry and Ron thought.

“我们想见邓布利多教授。”赫敏说，相当勇敢，哈利和罗恩想。

“See Professor Dumbledore?” Professor McGonagall repeated, as though this was a very fishy thing to want to do. “Why?”

“看到邓布利多教授?麦格教授重复道，仿佛这是一件很可疑的事。“为什么?”

Harry swallowed — now what?

哈利咽了口唾沫——现在怎么办?

“It’s sort of secret,” he said, but he wished at once he hadn’t, because Professor McGonagall’s nostrils flared.

“这是一种秘密，”他说，但他希望自己没有，因为麦格教授的鼻孔突然一亮。

“Professor Dumbledore left ten minutes ago,” she said coldly. “He received an urgent owl from the Ministry of Magic and flew off for London at once.”

“邓布利多教授十分钟前就走了，”她冷冷地说。“他收到魔法部紧急送来的猫头鹰，马上飞往伦敦。”

“He’s gone ?” said Harry frantically. “Now?”

“他走了?”“现在吗?”

“Professor Dumbledore is a very great wizard, Potter, he has many demands on his time—”

“邓布利多教授是一个非常伟大的巫师，波特，他对时间有很多要求——”

“But this is important.”

“但这很重要。”

“Something you have to say is more important than the Ministry of Magic, Potter?”

“你有话要说，比魔法部更重要吗，波特?”

“Look,” said Harry, throwing caution to the winds, “Professor — it’s about the Sorcerer’s Stone—”

“听着，”哈利把警告抛到九霄云外，“教授——是关于魔法石的——”

Whatever Professor McGonagall had expected, it wasn’t that. The books she was carrying tumbled out of her arms, but she didn’t pick them up.

不管麦格教授怎么想，事情并非如此。她手里拿的书从她怀里滚了出来，但她没有把它们捡起来。

“How do you know — ?” she spluttered.

“你怎么知道——”她结结巴巴地说。

“Professor, I think — I know — that Sn — that someone’s going to try and steal the Stone. I’ve got to talk to Professor Dumbledore.”

“教授，我想——我知道——那个Sn——有人要去偷那块石头。我得和邓布利多教授谈谈。

She eyed him with a mixture of shock and suspicion.

她既震惊又怀疑地看着他。

“Professor Dumbledore will be back tomorrow,” she said finally. I don’t know how you found out about the Stone, but rest assured, no one can possibly steal it, it’s too well protected.”

“邓布利多教授明天会回来的。”我不知道你是怎么知道那块石头的，但请放心，没有人能把它偷走的，它保护得太好了。”

“But Professor—”

“但是,教授”

“Potter, I know what I’m talking about,” she said shortly. She bent down and gathered up the fallen books. I suggest you all go back outside and enjoy the sunshine.”

“波特，我知道我在说什么，”她简短地说。她弯下腰把掉下来的书捡起来。我建议你们都到外面去享受阳光。”

But they didn’t.

但是他们没有。

“It’s tonight,” said Harry, once he was sure Professor McGonagall was out of earshot. “Snape’s going through the trapdoor tonight. He’s found out everything he needs, and now he’s got Dumbledore out of the way. He sent that note, I bet the Ministry of Magic will get a real shock when Dumbledore turns up.”

“就在今晚。”哈利一确定麦格教授不在听得见的地方，就说。“斯内普今晚要穿过活板门。”他已经找到了他需要的一切，现在他把邓布利多赶走了。他把信寄出去了，我敢打赌邓布利多一出现，魔法部一定会大吃一惊的。

“But what can we—”

“可是我们能——”

Hermione gasped. Harry and Ron wheeled round.

赫敏气喘吁吁地说。哈利和罗恩转过身来。

Snape was standing there.

斯内普站在那里。

“Good afternoon,” he said smoothly.

“下午好，”他平静地说。

They stared at him.

他们盯着他看。

“You shouldn’t be inside on a day like this,” he said, with an odd, twisted smile.

“你不应该在这样的日子里待在屋里，”他说，脸上带着奇怪的、扭曲的微笑。

“We were — ” Harry began, without any idea what he was going to say.

“我们——”哈利开始说，不知道自己要说什么。

“You want to be more careful,” said Snape. “Hanging around like this, people will think you’re up to something. And Gryffindor really can’t afford to lose any more points, can it?”

“你得小心点。”斯内普说。“像这样混在一起，人们会认为你在捣鬼。”格兰芬多真的不能再丢分了，是吗?”

Harry flushed. They turned to go outside, but Snape called them back.

哈利刷新。他们转身要出去，斯内普把他们叫了回来。

“Be warned, Potter — any more nighttime wanderings and I will personally make sure you are expelled. Good day to you.”

“警告你，波特——如果你再在夜间游荡，我会亲自把你开除的。祝你好运。”

He strode off in the direction of the staffroom.

他大步朝教工室的方向走去。

Out on the stone steps, Harry turned to the others.

哈利走到外面的石阶上，转向其他人。

“Right, here’s what we’ve got to do,” he whispered urgently. “One of us has got to keep an eye on Snape — wait outside the staff room and follow him if he leaves it. Hermione, you’d better do that.”

“好吧，这就是我们要做的，”他急切地低声说。“我们必须有一个人盯着斯内普——在教工室外面等着，如果他离开，就跟着他。”赫敏，你最好这么做。

“Why me?”

“为什么是我?”

“It’s obvious,” said Ron. “You can pretend to be waiting for Professor Flitwick, you know.” He put on a high voice, “’Oh Professor Flitwick, I’m so worried, I think I got question fourteen b wrong⋯ ‘”

“很明显，”罗恩说。“你可以假装在等弗立维教授，你知道的。”他穿上高的声音,“哦,弗立维教授,我好担心啊,我想我打错⋯问题14 b’”

“Oh, shut up,” said Hermione, but she agreed to go and watch out for Snape.

“哦，闭嘴。”赫敏说，但她同意去提防斯内普。

“And we’d better stay outside the third-floor corridor,” Harry told Ron. “Come on.”

“我们最好呆在三楼走廊外面，”哈利对罗恩说。“来吧。”

But that part of the plan didn’t work. No sooner had they reached the door separating Fluffy from the rest of the school than Professor McGonagall turned up again and this time, she lost her temper.

但这部分计划没有奏效。他们刚走到把毛毛和学校其他人隔开的门口，麦格教授又出现了。这一次，她发脾气了。

“I suppose you think you’re harder to get past than a pack of enchantments!” she stormed. “Enough of this nonsense! If I hear you’ve come anywhere near here again, I’ll take another fifty points from Gryffindor! Yes, Weasley, from my own house!”

“我想你认为你比一堆魔法更难对付吧!””她了。“别胡扯了!”如果我听说你又来了，我就要从格兰芬多再扣掉50分!“是的，韦斯莱，在我自己家里!

Harry and Ron went back to the common room, Harry had just said, “At least Hermione’s on Snape’s tail,” when the portrait of the Fat Lady swung open and Hermione came in.

赫敏长叹一声，哈利吃惊地发现她在笑，这是他最不愿意做的事。

“Brilliant,” said Hermione. “This isn’t magic — it’s logic — a puzzle. A lot of the greatest wizards haven’t got an ounce of logic, they’d be stuck in here forever.”

“聪明的“赫敏说。这不是魔法——这是逻辑——是个谜。许多最伟大的巫师都没有一点逻辑，他们会永远被困在这里。”

“But so will we, won’t we?”

“但是我们会的，不是吗?”

“Of course not,” said Hermione. “Everything we need is here on this paper. Seven bottles: three are poison; two are wine; one will get us safely through the black fire, and one will get us back through the purple.”

“当然不是，”赫敏说。“我们需要的一切都在这张纸上。七瓶:三瓶是毒药;两个酒;一个会让我们安全地穿过黑火，另一个会让我们穿过紫火。”

“But how do we know which to drink?”

“可是我们怎么知道喝什么呢?”

“Give me a minute.”

“给我一分钟。”

Hermione read the paper several times. Then she walked up and down the line of bottles, muttering to herself and pointing at them. At last, she clapped her hands.

赫敏把报纸读了好几遍。然后她在一排瓶子里走来走去，一边自言自语，一边指着瓶子。最后，她鼓起掌来。

“Got it,” she said. “The smallest bottle will get us through the black fire — toward the Stone.”

“明白了，”她说。“最小的瓶子也能让我们穿过黑火——朝着石头。”

Harry looked at the tiny bottle.

哈利看着那个小瓶子。

“There’s only enough there for one of us,” he said. “That’s hardly one swallow.”

“那里只够我们一个人吃，”他说。“那还不到一口呢。”

They looked at each other.

他们面面相觑。

“Which one will get you back through the purple flames?”

“哪一个能让你穿过紫色的火焰回来?”

Hermione pointed at a rounded bottle at the right end of the line.

赫敏指着队伍右边的一个圆瓶子。

“You drink that,” said Harry. “No, listen, get back and get Ron. Grab brooms from the flying-key room, they’ll get you out of the trapdoor and past Fluffy — go straight to the owlery and send Hedwig to Dumbledore, we need him. I might be able to hold Snape off for a while, but I’m no match for him, really.”

“你喝那个，”哈利说。“不，听着，回去找罗恩。”从飞行钥匙室拿把扫帚，他们会把你从活板门里弄出来，经过路威——直接去猫头鹰屋，把海德薇送到邓布利多那里，我们需要他。我也许能暂时阻止斯内普，但我真的不是他的对手。

“But Harry — what if You-Know-Who’s with him?”

“可是哈利——如果神秘人跟他在一起怎么办?”

“Well — I was lucky once, wasn’t I?” said Harry, pointing at his scar. “I might get lucky again.”

“嗯——我曾经很幸运，不是吗?”哈利指着自己的伤疤说。“我可能又会走运了。”

Hermione’s lip trembled, and she suddenly dashed at Harry and threw her arms around him.

赫敏的嘴唇颤抖着，她突然冲向哈利，伸出双臂搂住他。

“Hermione!”

“赫敏!”

“Harry — you’re a great wizard, you know.”

“哈利——你是一个伟大的巫师，你知道的。”

“I’m not as good as you,” said Harry, very embarrassed, as she let go of him.

“我没有你好，”哈利说，当她放开他时，他显得很尴尬。

“Me!” said Hermione. “Books! And cleverness! There are more important things — friendship and bravery and — oh Harry — be careful!”

“我!”赫敏说。“书!和聪明!还有更重要的事情——友谊和勇气，还有——哦，哈利——小心!”

“You drink first,” said Harry. “You are sure which is which, aren’t you?”

“你先喝吧。”“你肯定哪个是哪个，不是吗?”

“Positive,” said Hermione. She took a long drink from the round bottle at the end, and shuddered.

“积极的,”赫敏说。她从那只圆圆的瓶子里喝了一大口，打了个寒颤。

“It’s not poison?” said Harry anxiously.

“这不是毒药?哈利焦急地说。

“No — but it’s like ice.”

“没有——但是像冰一样。”

“Quick, go, before it wears off.”

“快走，趁它还没消失。”

“Good luck — take care.”

“祝你好运——保重。”

“GO!”

“走吧!”

Hermione turned and walked straight through the purple fire.

赫敏转过身，径直穿过紫色的火焰。

Harry took a deep breath and picked up the smallest bottle. He turned to face the black flames.

哈利深吸了一口气，拿起最小的瓶子。他转过身来面对着黑色的火焰。

“Here I come,” he said, and he drained the little bottle in one gulp.

“我来了，”他说着，一口气喝干了小瓶子里的水。

It was indeed as though ice was flooding his body. He put the bottle down and walked forward; he braced himself, saw the black flames licking his body, but couldn’t feel them — for a moment he could see nothing but dark fire — then he was on the other side, in the last chamber.

他的身体就像被冰淹没了一样。他放下酒瓶，向前走去;他打起精神，看见黑色的火焰舔着他的身体，但却感觉不到它们——有那么一会儿，他除了黑色的火焰什么也看不见——然后他就到了另一边，在最后一个房间里。

There was already someone there — but it wasn’t Snape. It wasn’t even Voldemort.

已经有人在那儿了——但不是斯内普。甚至不是伏地魔。

J.K. Rowling
