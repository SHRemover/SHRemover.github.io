Harry Potter and The Sorcerer’s Stone

Chapter Ten Halloween

第十章 万圣节

Malfoy couldn’t believe his eyes when he saw that Harry and Ron were still at Hogwarts the next day, looking tired but perfectly cheerful. Indeed, by the next morning Harry and Ron thought that meeting the three-headed dog had been an excellent adventure, and they were quite keen to have another one. In the meantime, Harry filled Ron in about the package that seemed to have been moved from Gringotts to Hogwarts, and they spent a lot of time wondering what could possibly need such heavy protection.

马尔福不敢相信自己的眼睛，第二天他看到哈利和罗恩还在霍格沃茨，虽然看上去很累，但非常高兴。的确，到了第二天早上，哈利和罗恩觉得见到这只三头狗是一次极好的冒险，他们非常想再来一次。与此同时，哈利把包裹从古灵阁搬到霍格沃茨的事告诉了罗恩，他们花了很多时间想知道，什么东西可能需要这么重的保护。

“It’s either really valuable or really dangerous,” said Ron.

“它要么非常值钱，要么非常危险，”罗恩说。

“Or both,” said Harry.

“或者两者都有。”

But as all they knew for sure about the mysterious object was that it was about two inches long, they didn’t have much chance of guessing what it was without further clues.

但是，由于他们所知道的关于这个神秘物体的全部确切信息是它大约有两英寸长，如果没有进一步的线索，他们猜不出它是什么东西的可能性不大。

Neither Neville nor Hermione showed the slightest interest in what lay underneath the dog and the trapdoor. All Neville cared about was never going near the dog again.

纳威和赫敏对藏在狗和活板门下面的东西都不感兴趣。纳威只关心再也不要靠近那条狗。

Hermione was now refusing to speak to Harry and Ron, but she was such a bossy know-it-all that they saw this as an added bonus. All they really wanted now was a way of getting back at Malfoy, and to their great delight, just such a thing arrived in the mail about a week later.

赫敏现在拒绝跟哈利和罗恩说话，但她是一个专横的万事通，他们把这看作是额外的奖励。他们现在真正想要的是找到一种报复马尔福的方法。令他们大为高兴的是，大约一个星期后，他们收到了一封邮件。

As the owls flooded into the Great Hall as usual, everyone’s attention was caught at once by a long, thin package carried by six large screech owls. Harry was just as interested as everyone else to see what was in this large parcel, and was amazed when the owls soared down and dropped it right in front of him, knocking his bacon to the floor. They had hardly fluttered out of the way when another owl dropped a letter on top of the parcel.

当猫头鹰像往常一样涌进大厅时，大家的注意力立刻被六只大尖猫头鹰提着的又长又细的包裹吸引住了。哈利和其他人一样，对这个大包里装的是什么东西很感兴趣。当猫头鹰飞下来，把它落在他面前，把他的熏肉撞倒在地时，哈利感到很吃惊。他们还没来得及闪开，另一只猫头鹰就把一封信落在了包裹上。

Harry ripped open the letter first, which was lucky, because it said:

哈利先撕开信，这很幸运，因为信上写着:

DO NOT OPEN THE PARCEL AT THE TABLE.

不要在餐桌上打开包裹。

It contains your new Nimbus Two Thousand, but I don’t want everybody knowing you’ve got a broomstick or they’ll all want one. Oliver Wood will meet you tonight on the Quidditch field at seven o’clock for your first training session.

这里边装的是你的新扫帚”光轮2000”，但我不想让每个人都知道你有飞天扫帚，否则他们都想要一把。奥利弗·伍德今晚7点在魁地奇球场和你碰面，开始你的第一次训练。

Professor McGonagall

麦格教授

Harry had difficulty hiding his glee as he handed the note to Ron to read.

哈利把纸条递给罗恩看时，难以掩饰自己的喜悦。

“A Nimbus Two Thousand!” Ron moaned enviously. “I’ve never even touched one.”

“光轮2000 !”罗恩羡慕地呻吟着。“我从没碰过。”

They left the hall quickly, wanting to unwrap the broomstick in private before their first class, but halfway across the entrance hall they found the way upstairs barred by Crabbe and Goyle. Malfoy seized the package from Harry and felt it.

他们很快地离开了大厅，想在第一节课开始前单独打开扫帚，但在门厅的半路上，他们发现楼上被克拉布和高尔挡住了去路。马尔福从哈利手里接过包裹，摸了摸。

“That’s a broomstick,” he said, throwing it back to Harry with a mixture of jealousy and spite on his face. “You’ll be in for it this time, Potter, first years aren’t allowed them.”

“那是把扫帚。”他说着把扫帚扔还给哈利，脸上混杂着嫉妒和怨恨。“这回你可要倒霉了，波特，一年级是不允许的。”

Ron couldn’t resist it.

罗恩实在忍不住了。

“It’s not any old broomstick,” he said, “it’s a Nimbus Two Thousand. What did you say you’ve got at home, Malfoy, a Comet Two Sixty?” Ron grinned at Harry. “Comets look flashy, but they’re not in the same league as the Nimbus.”

“这不是什么旧扫帚，”他说，“这是‘光轮2000’。”你说你家里有什么，马尔福，260彗星?罗恩冲哈利咧嘴一笑。”慧星’看起来也挺华丽的，可它们和’灵光’一比，哼，根本不是同一等级的嘛。

“What would you know about it, Weasley, you couldn’t afford half the handle,” Malfoy snapped back. “I suppose you and your brothers have to save up twig by twig.”

“你懂什么！威斯里，你恐怕连这个扫帚头都没有吧,”“我想你和你的兄弟们得一根一根地攒起来。”

Before Ron could answer, Professor Flitwick appeared at Malfoy’s elbow.

罗恩还没来得及回答，弗立维教授就出现在马尔福身边。

“Not arguing, I hope, boys?” he squeaked.

“我希望你们不要争论，孩子们?””他发出“吱吱”的响声。

“Potter’s been sent a broomstick, Professor,” said Malfoy quickly.

“教授，波特被送来了一把扫帚。”马尔福急忙说。

“Yes, yes, that’s right,” said Professor Flitwick, beaming at Harry. “Professor McGonagall told me all about the special circumstances, Potter. And what model is it?”

“是的，是的，说得对，”弗立维教授对哈利笑着说。“麦格教授把特殊情况都告诉了我，波特。它是什么模型?”

“A Nimbus Two Thousand, it is,” said Harry, fighting not to laugh at the look of horror on Malfoy’s face. “And it’s really thanks to Malfoy here that I’ve got it,” he added.

“是‘光轮2000’。”“多亏了马尔福，我才得到了它。”

Harry and Ron headed upstairs, smothering their laughter at Malfoy’s obvious rage and confusion.

哈利和罗恩朝楼上走去，对马尔福明显的愤怒和困惑忍住了笑声。

“Well, it’s true,” Harry chortled as they reached the top of the marble staircase, “If he hadn’t stolen Neville’s Remembrall I wouldn’t be on the team⋯”

“嗯,这是真的,”哈利乐不可支,因为他们达到大理石楼梯的顶端,“如果他没有偷了内维尔的记忆球,我也不会在球队⋯”

“So I suppose you think that’s a reward for breaking rules?” came an angry voice from just behind them. Hermione was stomping up the stairs, looking disapprovingly at the package in Harry’s hand.

“所以我想你认为这是对违反规定的奖励?”一个愤怒的声音从他们身后传来。赫敏跺着脚上了楼梯，不满地看着哈利手里的包裹。

“I thought you weren’t speaking to us?” said Harry.

“我还以为你不和我们说话呢?””哈利说。

“Yes, don’t stop now,” said Ron, “it’s doing us so much good.”

“是的，别停，”罗恩说，“这对我们太好了。”

Hermione marched away with her nose in the air.

赫敏昂首阔步地走开了。

Harry had a lot of trouble keeping his mind on his lessons that day. It kept wandering up to the dormitory where his new broomstick was lying under his bed, or straying off to the Quidditch field where he’d be learning to play that night. He bolted his dinner that evening without noticing what he was eating, and then rushed upstairs with Ron to unwrap the Nimbus Two Thousand at last.

那天，哈利费了好大劲才把注意力集中在功课上。它不停地跑到他的新扫帚放在床底下的宿舍，或者跑到他那天晚上要学打魁地奇球的场地。那天晚上，他狼吞虎咽地吃了一顿晚饭，根本没注意自己在吃什么，然后和罗恩一起冲上楼，终于打开了“光轮2000”。

“Wow,” Ron sighed, as the broomstick rolled onto Harry’s bedspread.

“哇，”罗恩叹了口气，扫帚滚到了哈利的床单上。

Even Harry, who knew nothing about the different brooms, thought it looked wonderful. Sleek and shiny, with a mahogany handle, it had a long tail of neat, straight twigs and Nimbus Two Thousand written in gold near the top.

就连对这些不同的扫帚一无所知的哈利也觉得它们看起来棒极了。它光滑而闪亮，有一个桃花心木把手，有一条长长的尾巴，尾巴上整齐笔直地插着细枝，顶部用金色写着“光轮2000”。

As seven o’clock drew nearer, Harry left the castle and set off in the dusk toward the Quidditch field. Held never been inside the stadium before. Hundreds of seats were raised in stands around the field so that the spectators were high enough to see what was going on. At either end of the field were three golden poles with hoops on the end. They reminded Harry of the little plastic sticks Muggle children blew bubbles through, except that they were fifty feet high.

七点钟越来越近了，哈利离开城堡，在暮色中向魁地奇球场走去。以前从未在体育场举行过。球场周围的看台上竖起了数百个座位，这样观众就能看到发生了什么事。在田野的两头各有三根金色的杆子，杆子的一头有铁箍。它们使哈利想起了麻瓜孩子们吹肥皂泡用的塑料小棍，只是它们有五十英尺高。

Too eager to fly again to wait for Wood, Harry mounted his broomstick and kicked off from the ground. What a feeling — he swooped in and out of the goal posts and then sped up and down the field. The Nimbus Two Thousand turned wherever he wanted at his lightest touch.

哈利迫不及待地想再飞起来，等不到伍德。他骑上扫帚，从地上踢了起来。这是一种多么美妙的感觉啊——他突然从球门冲了进来，又冲了出来，然后在球场上又跑上跑下。光轮2000只要他轻轻一碰，就会转到他想去的地方。

“Hey, Potter, come down!”

“嘿，波特，下来!”

Oliver Wood had arrived. He was carrying a large wooden crate under his arm. Harry landed next to him.

奥利弗·伍德已经到了。他腋下夹着一只大木箱。哈利在他旁边着陆。

“Very nice,” said Wood, his eyes glinting. “I see what McGonagall meant⋯ you really are a natural. I’m just going to teach you the rules this evening, then you’ll be joining team practice three times a week.”

“很好，”伍德说，他的眼睛闪闪发光。“我看到麦格是什么意思⋯你真是个自然。今天晚上我来教你们规则，然后你就可以每周参加三次小组练习了。”

He opened the crate. Inside were four different-sized balls.

他打开板条箱。里面有四个大小不同的球。

“Right,” said Wood. “Now, Quidditch is easy enough to understand, even if it’s not too easy to play. There are seven players on each side. Three of them are called Chasers.”

“没错,”伍德说。“现在，魁地奇很容易理解，即使它不太容易玩。双方各有七名队员。其中三个被称为追球者。

“Three Chasers,” Harry repeated, as Wood took out a bright red ball about the size of a soccer ball.

“三个追球手。”哈利重复道，伍德拿出一个足球大小的大红球。

“This ball’s called the Quaffle,” said Wood. “The Chasers throw the Quaffle to each other and try and get it through one of the hoops to score a goal. Ten points every time the Quaffle goes through one of the hoops. Follow me?”

“这个球叫鬼飞球，”伍德说。追球手们把鬼飞球扔给对方，然后试着让它穿过其中一个球门，进一球。每次鬼飞球经过一个篮圈就得10分。跟我来?”

“The Chasers throw the Quaffle and put it through the hoops to score,” Harry recited. “So — that’s sort of like basketball on broomsticks with six hoops, isn’t it?”

“追球手把鬼飞球扔出去，穿过球门得分。”哈利背诵道。“所以——这有点像骑着扫帚打篮球，带着六个篮筐，不是吗?”

“What’s basketball?” said Wood curiously.

“篮球是什么?伍德好奇地说。

“Never mind,” said Harry quickly.

“没关系，”哈利急忙说。

“Now, there’s another player on each side who’s called the Keeper — I’m Keeper for Gryffindor. I have to fly around our hoops and stop the other team from scoring.”

“现在，双方各有一名球员叫守门员——我是格兰芬多队的守门员。我得绕着我们的球门飞，阻止对方得分。”

“Three Chasers, one Keeper,” said Harry, who was determined to remember it all. “And they play with the Quaffle. Okay, got that. So what are they for?” He pointed at the three balls left inside the box.

“三个追球手，一个守门员。”哈利说，他决心把这一切都记住。他们和鬼飞球一起玩。好的,明白了。那么它们是干什么用的呢?他指着盒子里剩下的三个球。

“I’ll show you now,” said Wood. “Take this.”

“我现在给你看，”伍德说。“这个。”

He handed Harry a small club, a bit like a short baseball bat.

他递给哈利一支有点像短棒球棒的小棒子。

“I’m going to show you what the Bludgers do,” Wood said. “These two are the Bludgers.”

“我来告诉你游走球是干什么的，”伍德说。“这两个是游走球。”

He showed Harry two identical balls, jet black and slightly smaller than the red Quaffle. Harry noticed that they seemed to be straining to escape the straps holding them inside the box.

他给哈利看了两个一模一样的球，乌黑发亮，比红色的鬼飞球略小。哈利注意到它们似乎在拼命挣脱盒子里的皮带。

“Stand back,” Wood warned Harry. He bent down and freed one of the Bludgers.

“往后站。”伍德警告哈利。他弯下腰，放开了其中一只游走球。

At once, the black ball rose high in the air and then pelted straight at Harry’s face. Harry swung at it with the bat to stop it from breaking his nose, and sent it zigzagging away into the air — it zoomed around their heads and then shot at Wood, who dived on top of it and managed to pin it to the ground.

黑球立刻升到空中，然后径直朝哈利的脸砸去。哈利用球棒朝它挥去，不让它打断他的鼻子，然后让它曲折地飞向空中——它在他们的头上盘旋，然后向伍德开枪，伍德扑到球棒上，设法把球钉在地上。

“See?” Wood panted, forcing the struggling Bludger back into the crate and strapping it down safely. “The Bludgers rocket around, trying to knock players off their brooms. That’s why you have two Beaters on each team — the Weasley twins are ours — it’s their job to protect their side from the Bludgers and try and knock them toward the other team. So — think you’ve got all that?”

“看到了吗?伍德气喘吁吁地说，把挣扎着的游走球推回到板条箱里，用带子把它捆好。游走球飞来飞去，试图把球员们从扫帚上撞下来。这就是为什么每个队都有两个击球手——韦斯莱双胞胎是我们的——他们的工作就是保护自己的球队不被游走球击中，然后把他们撞向另一个队。那么——你认为你已经具备了所有这些条件吗?”

“Three Chasers try and score with the Quaffle; the Keeper guards the goal posts; the Beaters keep the Bludgers away from their team,” Harry reeled off.

“三个追球手试图用鬼飞球得分;守门员看守球门柱;击球手使游走球远离他们的队伍。

“Very good,” said Wood.

“很好，”伍德说。

“Er — have the Bludgers ever killed anyone?” Harry asked, hoping he sounded offhand.

“呃——游走球杀过人吗?”哈利问，希望他的话听起来很随便。

“Never at Hogwarts. We’ve had a couple of broken jaws but nothing worse than that. Now, the last member of the team is the Seeker. That’s you. And you don’t have to worry about the Quaffle or the Bludgers—”

“在霍格瓦彻学校里还没发生过这种事。我们有过几次下巴骨折，但没有比这更糟糕的了。现在，最后一个队员是探索者。这是你的。你不用担心鬼飞球和游走球

“— unless they crack my head open.”

“除非他们把我的头敲开。”

“Don’t worry, the Weasleys are more than a match for the Bludgers — I mean, they’re like a pair of human Bludgers themselves.”

“别担心，韦斯莱兄弟比游走球强多了——我是说，他们自己就像一对游走球。”

Wood reached into the crate and took out the fourth and last ball. Compared with the Quaffle and the Bludgers, it was tiny, about the size of a large walnut. It was bright gold and had little fluttering silver wings.

伍德把手伸进板条箱，拿出了第四个也是最后一个球。与鬼飞球和游走球相比，它很小，大约有一个大核桃那么大。它是明亮的金色，有银色的小翅膀。

“This,” said Wood, “is the Golden Snitch, and it’s the most important ball of the lot. It’s very hard to catch because it’s so fast and difficult to see. It’s the Seeker’s job to catch it. You’ve got to weave in and out of the Chasers, Beaters, Bludgers, and Quaffle to get it before the other team’s Seeker, because whichever Seeker catches the Snitch wins his team an extra hundred and fifty points, so they nearly always win. That’s why Seekers get fouled so much. A game of Quidditch only ends when the Snitch is caught, so it can go on for ages — I think the record is three months, they had to keep bringing on substitutes so the players could get some sleep.

“这个，”伍德说，“是金色飞贼，它是所有球中最重要的一个。它非常难接住，因为它跑得太快，很难看清。”探索者的工作就是抓住它。你必须在追球手、击球手、游走球和鬼飞球之间来回穿梭，才能在对方的找球手之前拿到球，因为无论哪位找球手抓到金色飞贼，他的球队就能多得150分，所以他们几乎总是赢。这也是为什么要找到一个优秀的搜索者会那么困难。一场魁地奇比赛只有在金色飞贼被抓住的时候才会结束，所以这种情况可能会持续很长时间——我想纪录是三个月，他们不得不不停地换上替补，这样球员们才能睡一会儿。

“Well, that’s it, any questions?”

“好了，就这些了，有什么问题吗?”

Harry shook his head. He understood what he had to do all right, it was doing it that was going to be the problem.

哈利摇摇头。他明白他必须做什么好吧，这就是问题所在。

“We won’t practice with the Snitch yet,” said Wood, carefully shutting it back inside the crate, “it’s too dark, we might lose it. Let’s try you out with a few of these.”

“我们还不打算和金色飞贼一起训练，”伍德说着，小心翼翼地把它关进板条箱里，“天太黑了，我们可能会把它弄丢的。”让我们用其中的一些来试试你。”

He pulled a bag of ordinary golf balls out of his pocket and a few minutes later, he and Harry were up in the air, Wood throwing the golf balls as hard as he could in every direction for Harry to catch.

他从口袋里掏出一袋普通的高尔夫球，几分钟后，他和哈利飞到了空中，伍德用尽全力朝各个方向扔高尔夫球，让哈利接住。

Harry didn’t miss a single one, and Wood was delighted. After half an hour, night had really fallen and they couldn’t carry on.

哈利一个也没错过，伍德很高兴。半小时后，天真的黑了，他们无法继续了。

“That Quidditch Cup’ll have our name on it this year,” said Wood happily as they trudged back up to the castle. “I wouldn’t be surprised if you turn out better than Charlie Weasley, and he could have played for England if he hadn’t gone off chasing dragons.”

“今年魁地奇杯上要刻上我们的名字了。”他们步履蹒跚地回到城堡，伍德高兴地说。“如果你比查理·韦斯莱表现得更好，我不会感到惊讶。如果他没有去追龙的话，他本来可以为英格兰队效力的。”

Perhaps it was because he was now so busy, what with Quidditch practice three evenings a week on top of all his homework, but Harry could hardly believe it when he realized that he’d already been at Hogwarts two months. The castle felt more like home than Privet Drive ever had. His lessons, too, were becoming more and more interesting now that they had mastered the basics.

也许是因为他现在太忙了，每周有三个晚上在做作业的同时还要练习魁地奇，但是当哈利意识到他已经在霍格沃茨两个月了，他简直不敢相信。这座城堡比女贞路更像一个家。他的课也变得越来越有趣，因为他们已经掌握了基础知识。

On Halloween morning they woke to the delicious smell of baking pumpkin wafting through the corridors. Even better, Professor Flitwick announced in Charms that he thought they were ready to start making objects fly, something they had all been dying to try since they’d seen him make Neville’s toad zoom around the classroom. Professor Flitwick put the class into pairs to practice. Harry’s partner was Seamus Finnigan (which was a relief, because Neville had been trying to catch his eye). Ron, however, was to be working with Hermione Granger. It was hard to tell whether Ron or Hermione was angrier about this. She hadn’t spoken to either of them since the day Harry’s broomstick had arrived.

赫敏低下头。哈里是说不出话来。赫敏是最不可能违犯校规的人，而她却假装违犯了校规，让他们摆脱了麻烦。斯内普好像已经开始分发糖果了。

“Miss Granger, five points will be taken from Gryffindor for this,” said Professor McGonagall. “I’m very disappointed in you. If you’re not hurt at all, you’d better get off to Gryffindor tower. Students are finishing the feast in their houses.”

“格兰杰小姐，为此格兰芬多要扣掉五分。”麦格教授说。“我对你很失望。如果你没有受伤，你最好去格兰芬多塔。学生们正在他们的家里吃大餐。

Hermione left.

赫敏离开了。

Professor McGonagall turned to Harry and Ron.

麦格教授转向哈利和罗恩。

“Well, I still say you were lucky, but not many first years could have taken on a full-grown mountain troll. You each win Gryffindor five points. Professor Dumbledore will be informed of this. You may go.”

“好吧，我还是说你很幸运，但在最初的几年里，你不可能遇到一个成年的山怪。”你们每人为格兰芬多赢得5分。邓布利多教授会知道的。你可以走了。”

They hurried out of the chamber and didn’t speak at all until they had climbed two floors up. It was a relief to be away from the smell of the troll, quite apart from anything else.

他们匆匆走出房间，直到上了两层楼才开口说话。远离巨魔的气味，远离其他任何东西，这是一种解脱。

“We should have gotten more than ten points,” Ron grumbled.

“我们应该得10分以上。”罗恩抱怨道。

“Five, you mean, once she’s taken off Hermione’s.”

“只有5分。因为赫敏被老师扣了5分。”

“Good of her to get us out of trouble like that,” Ron admitted. “Mind you, we did save her.”

“她真好，让我们摆脱了那样的麻烦。”“请注意，我们确实救了她。”

“She might not have needed saving if we hadn’t locked the thing in with her,” Harry reminded him.

“如果我们没有把那东西锁在她身边，她可能就不需要救了。”哈利提醒他。

They had reached the portrait of the Fat Lady.

他们走到胖夫人的画像跟前。

“Pig snout,” they said and entered.

“猪鼻子，”他们说着走了进去。

The common room was packed and noisy. Everyone was eating the food that had been sent up. Hermione, however, stood alone by the door, waiting for them. There was a very embarrassed pause. Then, none of them looking at each other, they all said “Thanks,” and hurried off to get plates.

公共休息室里挤满了人，很吵。每个人都在吃送来的食物。然而，赫敏独自站在门口，等着他们。一阵尴尬的沉默。然后，他们谁也没有看对方一眼，都说了声“谢谢”，然后急忙去拿盘子。

But from that moment on, Hermione Granger became their friend. There are some things you can’t share without ending up liking each other, and knocking out a twelve-foot mountain troll is one of them.

但从那一刻起，赫敏格兰杰成了他们的朋友。有些事情你不能和别人分享，但最终你们会喜欢上对方，击倒一个12英尺高的巨魔就是其中之一。

J.K. Rowling

J.K.罗琳
