Harry Potter and The Sorcerer’s Stone

Chapter Seven The Sorting Hat

第七章 分院帽

The door swung open at once. A tall, black-haired witch in emerald-green robes stood there. She had a very stern face and Harry’s first thought was that this was not someone to cross.

门立刻开了。站在那里的是一个高高的黑发女巫，穿着墨绿色的长袍。她的脸很严肃，哈利的第一个念头是要通过她的课可能很难。

“The firs’ years, Professor McGonagall,” said Hagrid.

“是第一学年，麦格教授。”海格说。

“Thank you, Hagrid. I will take them from here.”

“谢谢你,海格。我要把他们从这里带走。

She pulled the door wide. The entrance hall was so big you could have fit the whole of the Dursleys’ house in it. The stone walls were lit with flaming torches like the ones at Gringotts, the ceiling was too high to make out, and a magnificent marble staircase facing them led to the upper floors.

她把门开得很大。入口大厅大得可以装得下德思礼一家的全部房子。石墙上点着像古灵阁那样熊熊燃烧的火把，天花板高得看不清，对面有一座宏伟的大理石楼梯通往楼上。

They followed Professor McGonagall across the flagged stone floor. Harry could hear the drone of hundreds of voices from a doorway to the right — the rest of the school must already be here — but Professor McGonagall showed the first years into a small, empty chamber off the hall. They crowded in, standing rather closer together than they would usually have done, peering about nervously.

他们跟着麦格教授穿过铺着石板的地板。哈利能听到从右边门口传来的几百个低沉的声音——学校的其他人肯定已经到了——但麦格教授把新生们带进了大厅外一间空无一人的小房间。他们挤了进来，站得比平时更近了，紧张地四处张望。

“Welcome to Hogwarts,” said Professor McGonagall. “The start-of-term banquet will begin shortly, but before you take your seats in the Great Hall, you will be sorted into your houses. The Sorting is a very important ceremony because, while you are here, your house will be something like your family within Hogwarts. You will have classes with the rest of your house, sleep in your house dormitory, and spend free time in your house common room.

“欢迎来到霍格沃茨。”麦格教授说。开学宴会马上就要开始了，但是在你们进入礼堂就座之前，你们将被分到自己的房间里。分院是一个非常重要的仪式，因为当你在这里的时候，你的房子就像你在霍格沃茨的家人一样。你将和你的房子的其他部分一起上课，睡在你的房子宿舍，在你的房子公共休息室度过空闲时间。

“The four houses are called Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin. Each house has its own noble history and each has produced outstanding witches and wizards. While you are at Hogwarts, your triumphs will earn your house points, while any rulebreaking will lose house points. At the end of the year, the house with the most points is awarded the house cup, a great honor. I hope each of you will be a credit to whichever house becomes yours.

这四个学院分别叫做格兰芬多、赫奇帕奇、拉文克劳和斯莱特林。每家都有自己高贵的历史，每家都出了杰出的男女巫师。当你在霍格沃茨的时候，你的胜利会给你加分，而任何违反规则的行为都会被扣分。在年底，学院获得的积分最多的是学院杯，这是一个很大的荣誉。我希望你们每个人都能为你们的学院增光添彩。

“The Sorting Ceremony will take place in a few minutes in front of the rest of the school. I suggest you all smarten yourselves up as much as you can while you are waiting.”

分院仪式几分钟后将在全校师生面前举行。我建议你们在等候的时候，尽量打扮一下自己。”

Her eyes lingered for a moment on Neville’s cloak, which was fastened under his left ear, and on Ron’s smudged nose. Harry nervously tried to flatten his hair.

她的目光在纳威的斗篷上停留了一会儿，那件斗篷夹在他的左耳下面，还有罗恩那脏兮兮的鼻子上。哈利紧张地想把头发弄平。

“I shall return when we are ready for you,” said Professor McGonagall. “Please wait quietly.”

“我们准备好了就回来。”麦格教授说。“请安静地等候。”

She left the chamber. Harry swallowed.

她离开了房间。哈里吞下。

“How exactly do they sort us into houses?” he asked Ron.

“他们是怎么把我们分门别类的?”他问罗恩。

“Some sort of test, I think. Fred said it hurts a lot, but I think he was joking.”

“我想是某种考验吧。弗雷德说很疼，但我想他是在开玩笑。”

Harry’s heart gave a horrible jolt. A test? In front of the whole school? But he didn’t know any magic yet — what on earth would he have to do? He hadn’t expected something like this the moment they arrived. He looked around anxiously and saw that everyone else looked terrified, too. No one was talking much except Hermione Granger, who was whispering very fast about all the spells she’d learned and wondering which one she’d need. Harry tried hard not to listen to her. He’d never been more nervous, never, not even when he’d had to take a school report home to the Dursleys saying that he’d somehow turned his teacher’s wig blue. He kept his eyes fixed on the door. Any second now, Professor McGonagall would come back and lead him to his doom.

哈利的心猛地一震。一个测试吗?当着全校的面?但是他还不知道任何魔法——他究竟要做什么呢?他们一到，他就没想到会发生这样的事。他不安地环顾四周，发现其他人看起来也很害怕。除了赫敏格兰杰，没人多说话。她飞快地低声说着她学过的咒语，不知道自己需要什么咒语。哈利竭力不听她的话。他从来没有像现在这样紧张过，从来没有像现在这样紧张过，甚至当他不得不把一份成绩单带回德思礼家说他不知怎么把老师的假发染成了蓝色的时候。他目不转睛地盯着门。现在，麦格教授随时都可能回来，把他引向毁灭。

Then something happened that made him jump about a foot in the air — several people behind him screamed.

然后发生了一件事，使他在空中跳了大约一英尺——他身后的几个人尖叫起来。

“What the — ?”

“什么——?”

He gasped. So did the people around him. About twenty ghosts had just streamed through the back wall. Pearly-white and slightly transparent, they glided across the room talking to one another and hardly glancing at the first years. They seemed to be arguing. What looked like a fat little monk was saying: “Forgive and forget, I say, we ought to give him a second chance—”

他气喘吁吁地说。他周围的人也是如此。大约有二十个鬼魂刚刚从后墙里窜了出来。他们的脸白得像珍珠一样，有点透明，他们在房间里踱来踱去，彼此交谈着，头几年几乎不看一眼。他们似乎在争吵。一个胖胖的小和尚在说:“原谅他，忘记他，我说，我们应该给他第二次机会——”

“My dear Friar, haven’t we given Peeves all the chances he deserves? He gives us all a bad name and you know, he’s not really even a ghost — I say, what are you all doing here?”

“我亲爱的修士，难道我们没有给皮皮鬼应有的一切机会吗?”他给我们大家带来了坏名声，你知道，他实际上连个鬼都不是——我说，你们在这儿干什么?”

A ghost wearing a ruff and tights had suddenly noticed the first years.

一个戴着领结、穿着紧身衣的鬼魂突然注意到了新生们。

Nobody answered.

没有人回答。

“New students!” said the Fat Friar, smiling around at them. “About to be Sorted, I suppose?”

“新同学!胖修士微笑着说。“我想是要被分类了吧?”

A few people nodded mutely.

有几个人默默地点了点头。

“Hope to see you in Hufflepuff!” said the Friar. “My old house, you know.”

“希望在赫奇帕奇见到你!””修士说。“我的老房子，你知道。”

“Move along now,” said a sharp voice. “The Sorting Ceremony’s about to start.”

“走吧，”一个尖利的声音说。“分院仪式就要开始了。”

Professor McGonagall had returned. One by one, the ghosts floated away through the opposite wall.

麦格教授回来了。鬼魂们一个接一个地从对面的墙上飘走了。

“Now, form a line,” Professor McGonagall told the first years, “and follow me.”

“现在，排成一排，”麦格教授对新生们说，“跟我来。”

Feeling oddly as though his legs had turned to lead, Harry got into line behind a boy with sandy hair, with Ron behind him, and they walked out of the chamber, back across the hall, and through a pair of double doors into the Great Hall.

哈利感到很奇怪，他的腿好像变成了领头的，他跟在一个沙色头发的男孩后面排好队，罗恩跟在他后面。

Harry had never even imagined such a strange and splendid place. It was lit by thousands and thousands of candles that were floating in midair over four long tables, where the rest of the students were sitting. These tables were laid with glittering golden plates and goblets. At the top of the hall was another long table where the teachers were sitting. Professor McGonagall led the first years up here, so that they came to a halt in a line facing the other students, with the teachers behind them. The hundreds of faces staring at them looked like pale lanterns in the flickering candlelight. Dotted here and there among the students, the ghosts shone misty silver. Mainly to avoid all the staring eyes, Harry looked upward and saw a velvety black ceiling dotted with stars. He heard Hermione whisper, “Its bewitched to look like the sky outside. I read about it in Hogwarts, A History.”

哈利从来没有想到会有这样一个奇怪而又美丽的地方。它被成千上万的蜡烛照亮，这些蜡烛漂浮在四张长桌子的半空中，其他的学生都坐在桌子上。这些桌子上摆着闪闪发光的金盘子和高脚杯。在大厅的顶部是另一张长桌子，老师们正坐在那里。麦康娜教授把一年级的学生带到这里来，所以他们面对着其他学生排成一列，停了下来，后面跟着老师。数以百计的人盯着他们看，就像闪烁的烛光下苍白的灯笼。在学生们中间，鬼魂们散布在各处，发出朦胧的银光。哈利抬头一看，只见天鹅绒般的黑色天花板上星星点点。他听见赫敏低声说:“像外面的天空一样是被施了魔法的。我在《霍格沃茨的历史》中读到过。

It was hard to believe there was a ceiling there at all, and that the Great Hall didn’t simply open on to the heavens.

很难相信那里有一个天花板，而且大殿并不是简单地对着天空开放的。

Harry quickly looked down again as Professor McGonagall silently placed a four-legged stool in front of the first years. On top of the stool she put a pointed wizard’s hat. This hat was patched and frayed and extremely dirty. Aunt Petunia wouldn’t have let it in the house.

当麦格教授默默地把一张四脚凳放在新生面前时，哈利迅速地低下头来。她在凳子上放了一顶尖尖的巫师帽。这顶帽子打了补丁，又破又脏。佩妮姨妈不会让它进屋子的。

Maybe they had to try and get a rabbit out of it, Harry thought wildly, that seemed the sort of thing — noticing that everyone in the hall was now staring at the hat, he stared at it, too. For a few seconds, there was complete silence. Then the hat twitched. A rip near the brim opened wide like a mouth — and the hat began to sing:

“也许他们得想办法从帽子里弄出一只兔子来，”哈利狂乱地想，“好像是这样的——他注意到大厅里的每个人都在盯着帽子看，他也盯着帽子看。”有几秒钟，一片寂静。然后帽子抽动了一下。帽沿附近的一条缝裂开了，像张大嘴——帽子唱起歌来:

“Oh, you may not think I’m pretty,

“哦，你可能不认为我漂亮，

But don’t judge on what you see,

但不要根据你看到的来判断，

I’ll eat myself if you can find

如果你能找到，我就自己吃

A smarter hat than me.

比我聪明的帽子。

You can keep your bowlers black,

你可以让你的保龄球保持黑色，

你的高帽子又高又光滑，

For I’m the Hogwarts Sorting Hat

因为我是霍格沃茨分院帽

分院帽看不见，

试试我，我会告诉你的

你可能属于格兰芬多，

他们的勇敢、勇气和骑士精神

因为我是一顶会思考的帽子!”

The whole hall burst into applause as the hat finished its song. It bowed to each of the four tables and then became quite still again.

帽子唱完歌，全场爆发出热烈的掌声。它向四张桌子各鞠了一躬，然后又静了下来。

“So we’ve just got to try on the hat!” Ron whispered to Harry. “I’ll kill Fred, he was going on about wrestling a troll.”

“所以我们得试试这顶帽子!”罗恩小声对哈利说。“我要杀了弗雷德，他正在和巨魔摔跤呢。”

Harry smiled weakly. Yes, trying on the hat was a lot better than having to do a spell, but he did wish they could have tried it on without everyone watching. The hat seemed to be asking rather a lot; Harry didn’t feel brave or quick-witted or any of it at the moment. If only the hat had mentioned a house for people who felt a bit queasy, that would have been the one for him.

哈利微微一笑。是的，戴上这顶帽子比施魔法好多了，但他真希望他们能在没有人观看的情况下戴上它。帽子似乎问了很多问题;哈利此刻觉得自己既不勇敢，也不机智。如果这顶帽子提到的房子是给那些感觉有点恶心的人住的，那就太适合他了。

Professor McGonagall now stepped forward holding a long roll of parchment.

麦格教授这时走上前来，手里拿着一卷羊皮纸。

“When I call your name, you will put on the hat and sit on the stool to be sorted,” she said. “Abbott, Hannah!”

她说:“当我叫到你的名字时，你就把帽子戴上，坐在凳子上整理。”“阿伯特,汉娜!”

A pink-faced girl with blonde pigtails stumbled out of line, put on the hat, which fell right down over her eyes, and sat down. A moments pause —

一个粉红脸蛋、扎着金色马尾辫的女孩从队伍里跌跌撞撞地走了出来，戴上帽子，帽子正好落在她的眼睛上，她坐了下来。片刻的停顿-

“HUFFLEPUFF!” shouted the hat.

“赫奇帕奇!帽子喊道。

The table on the right cheered and clapped as Hannah went to sit down at the Hufflepuff table. Harry saw the ghost of the Fat Friar waving merrily at her.

当汉娜走到赫奇帕奇的桌旁坐下时，右边的桌子上响起了欢呼声和掌声。哈利看见胖修士的鬼魂高兴地向她挥手。

“Bones, Susan!”

“骨头,苏珊!”

“HUFFLEPUFF!” shouted the hat again, and Susan scuttled off to sit next to Hannah.

“赫奇帕奇!帽子又叫了一声，苏珊急忙跑开，坐在汉娜的旁边。

“Boot, Terry!”

“引导,特里!”

“拉文克劳!”

The table second from the left clapped this time; several Ravenclaws stood up to shake hands with Terry as he joined them.

左边第二张桌子这次拍手;当特里加入他们时，几个拉文克劳人站起来和他握手。

“Brocklehurst, Mandy” went to Ravenclaw too, but “Brown, Lavender” became the first new Gryffindor, and the table on the far left exploded with cheers; Harry could see Ron’s twin brothers catcalling.

“布罗克赫斯特，曼迪”也去了拉文克劳，但是“布朗，拉文德”成了格兰芬多的第一个新生，最左边的桌子上爆发出一阵欢呼声;哈利看见罗恩的孪生兄弟在叫他。

“Bulstrode, Millicent” then became a Slytherin. Perhaps it was Harry’s imagination, after all he’d heard about Slytherin, but he thought they looked like an unpleasant lot.

“布尔斯特罗德，米莉森特”后来成了斯莱特林人。也许这是哈利的想象，毕竟他听说过斯莱特林，但他觉得他们看起来很不讨人喜欢。

He was starting to feel definitely sick now. He remembered being picked for teams during gym at his old school. He had always been last to be chosen, not because he was no good, but because no one wanted Dudley to think they liked him.

他现在开始觉得很不舒服。他记得在他以前的学校里，他在体育课上被选为队员。他总是最后一个被选中，不是因为他不好，而是因为没有人希望达力认为他们喜欢他。

“Finch-Fletchley, Justin!”

“Finch-Fletchley,贾斯汀!”

“HUFFLEPUFF!”

“赫奇帕奇!”

Sometimes, Harry noticed, the hat shouted out the house at once, but at others it took a little while to decide. “Finnigan, Seamus,” the sandy-haired boy next to Harry in the line, sat on the stool for almost a whole minute before the hat declared him a Gryffindor.

哈利注意到，有时帽子会立刻把屋子里的人都喊出来，但有时又过了一会儿才做出决定。“芬尼根，西莫，”队伍里哈利旁边那个沙头发的男孩在凳子上坐了将近一分钟，帽子才宣布他是格兰芬多。

“Granger, Hermione!”

“格兰杰,赫敏!”

Hermione almost ran to the stool and jammed the hat eagerly on her head.

赫敏几乎跑到凳子上，迫不及待地把帽子塞到头上。

“GRYFFINDOR!” shouted the hat. Ron groaned.

“格兰芬多!帽子喊道。罗恩呻吟着。

A horrible thought struck Harry, as horrible thoughts always do when you’re very nervous. What if he wasn’t chosen at all? What if he just sat there with the hat over his eyes for ages, until Professor McGonagall jerked it off his head and said there had obviously been a mistake and he’d better get back on the train?

哈利突然想到了一个可怕的念头，就像你紧张的时候经常想到的那样。如果他根本没有被选中呢?如果他一直戴着帽子坐在那里，直到麦格教授突然把帽子从头上摘下来，说他肯定是弄错了，最好还是回到火车上，那该怎么办?

When Neville Longbottom, the boy who kept losing his toad, was called, he fell over on his way to the stool. The hat took a long time to decide with Neville. When it finally shouted, “GRYFFINDOR,” Neville ran off still wearing it, and had to jog back amid gales of laughter to give it to “MacDougal, Morag.”

当不断失去癞蛤蟆的纳威·隆巴顿被叫来的时候，他在去凳子的路上摔倒了。帽子花了很长时间才和纳威商量好。最后它叫道:“格兰芬多。”纳威仍然穿着它跑开了，在一阵哄堂大笑中，他不得不慢慢地跑回来，把它给了“麦克道戈尔，莫拉格。”

Malfoy swaggered forward when his name was called and got his wish at once: the hat had barely touched his head when it screamed, “SLYTHERIN!”

当叫到马尔福的名字时，他大摇大摆地走上前，立刻实现了自己的愿望:帽子刚碰到他的头，它就尖叫起来:“斯莱特林!”

Malfoy went to join his friends Crabbe and Goyle, looking pleased with himself.

马尔福走到他的朋友克拉布和高尔身边，看上去很高兴。

There weren’t many people left now. “Moon”⋯, “Nott”⋯, “Parkinson”⋯, then a pair of twin girls, “Patil” and “Patil”⋯, then “Perks, Sally-Anne”⋯, and then, at last —

现在已经没有多少人了。“月亮”⋯⋯“诺特”,“帕金森”⋯,然后一对双胞胎女儿,“帕蒂尔”和“帕蒂尔”⋯,然后“福利,sally anne”⋯,然后,最后

“Potter, Harry!”

“波特,哈利!”

As Harry stepped forward, whispers suddenly broke out like little hissing fires all over the hall.

哈利走上前去时，突然传来一阵窃窃私语，像一堆嘶嘶作响的小火，传遍了大厅。

“Potter, did she say?”

“波特，她说什么?”

“The Harry Potter?”

“《哈利·波特》?”

The Gryffindor first years followed Percy through the chattering crowds, out of the Great Hall, and up the marble staircase. Harry’s legs were like lead again, but only because he was so tired and full of food. He was too sleepy even to be surprised that the people in the portraits along the corridors whispered and pointed as they passed, or that twice Percy led them through doorways hidden behind sliding panels and hanging tapestries. They climbed more staircases, yawning and dragging their feet, and Harry was just wondering how much farther they had to go when they came to a sudden halt.

一年级的格兰芬多学生跟着珀西穿过叽叽喳喳的人群，走出大会堂，走上大理石楼梯。哈利的腿又像铅了，但那只是因为他太累了，又吃饱了。他太困了，连走廊上挂着画像的人经过时都在低声指指点点，或者珀西两次领着他们穿过隐藏在滑动嵌板和挂毯后面的门廊，他都不觉得奇怪。他们又爬了几级楼梯，打着呵欠，拖着脚。哈利正在纳闷他们还得走多远，突然停了下来。

A bundle of walking sticks was floating in midair ahead of them, and as Percy took a step toward them they started throwing themselves at him.

一捆拐杖漂浮在他们前面的半空中，珀西向他们迈了一步，他们开始扑向他。

“Peeves,” Percy whispered to the first years. “A poltergeist.” He raised his voice, “Peeves — show yourself.”

“皮皮鬼，”珀西对新生们低声说。“吵闹鬼。他提高了声音，“皮皮鬼——快出来。”

A loud, rude sound, like the air being let out of a balloon, answered.

一个响亮而粗鲁的声音，就像气球里的空气，回答道。

“Do you want me to go to the Bloody Baron?”

“你要我去血男爵那儿吗?”

There was a pop, and a little man with wicked, dark eyes and a wide mouth appeared, floating cross-legged in the air, clutching the walking sticks.

砰的一声，一个长着邪恶的黑眼睛和大嘴巴的小个子男人出现了，盘腿浮在空中，手里抓着拐杖。

“Oooooooh!” he said, with an evil cackle. “Ickle Firsties! What fun!”

“Oooooooh !他邪恶地咯咯笑着说。“Ickle Firsties !什么乐趣!”

He swooped suddenly at them. They all ducked.

他突然向他们猛扑过去。他们都回避。

“Go away, Peeves, or the Baron’ll hear about this, I mean it!” barked Percy.

“走开，皮皮鬼，不然男爵会听到这件事的，我是说真的!”“叫珀西。

Peeves stuck out his tongue and vanished, dropping the walking sticks on Neville’s head. They heard him zooming away, rattling coats of armor as he passed.

皮皮鬼伸出舌头不见了，手杖掉在纳威的头上。他们听见他飞驰而去，走过时铠甲格格作响。

“You want to watch out for Peeves,” said Percy, as they set off again. “The Bloody Baron’s the only one who can control him, he won’t even listen to us prefects. Here we are.”

“你要当心皮皮鬼，”珀西说着，他们又出发了。“血腥男爵是唯一能控制他的人，他甚至不听我们长官的话。”我们在这里。”

At the very end of the corridor hung a portrait of a very fat woman in a pink silk dress.

在走廊的尽头挂着一幅画，画上是一个穿着粉红色丝绸衣服的胖女人。

“Password?” she said.

“密码?”她说。

“Caput Draconis,” said Percy, and the portrait swung forward to reveal a round hole in the wall. They all scrambled through it — Neville needed a leg up — and found themselves in the Gryffindor common room, a cozy, round room full of squashy armchairs.

“德古尼斯角。”珀西说着，画像向前一摆，露出墙上的一个圆洞。他们都爬了过去——纳威需要站起来——发现自己来到了格兰芬多公共休息室，一个舒适的圆形房间，里面摆满了皱巴巴的扶手椅。

Percy directed the girls through one door to their dormitory and the boys through another. At the top of a spiral staircase — they were obviously in one of the towers — they found their beds at last: five four-posters hung with deep red, velvet curtains. Their trunks had already been brought up. Too tired to talk much, they pulled on their pajamas and fell into bed.

珀西引导女孩们从一扇门进入宿舍，男孩们从另一扇门进入。在螺旋形楼梯的顶端，他们终于找到了自己的床:五张四柱床，挂着深红色天鹅绒窗帘。他们的箱子已经搬上来了。他们累得说不出话来，便穿上睡衣上床睡觉了。

“Great food, isn’t it?” Ron muttered to Harry through the hangings. “Get off, Scabbers! He’s chewing my sheets.”

“很棒的食物，不是吗?”罗恩透过衣架小声对哈利说。“下车,斑斑!他在嚼我的床单。”

Harry was going to ask Ron if he’d had any of the treacle tart, but he fell asleep almost at once.

哈利正要问罗恩有没有吃糖浆馅饼，但他几乎马上就睡着了。

Perhaps Harry had eaten a bit too much, because he had a very strange dream. He was wearing Professor Quirrell’s turban, which kept talking to him, telling him he must transfer to Slytherin at once, because it was his destiny. Harry told the turban he didn’t want to be in Slytherin; it got heavier and heavier; he tried to pull it off but it tightened painfully — and there was Malfoy, laughing at him as he struggled with it — then Malfoy turned into the hook-nosed teacher, Snape, whose laugh became high and cold — there was a burst of green light and Harry woke, sweating and shaking.

也许哈利吃得太多了，因为他做了一个很奇怪的梦。他戴着奇洛教授的头巾，上面不停地跟他说话，告诉他必须马上转到斯莱特林去，因为这是他的命运。哈利告诉头巾，他不想呆在斯莱特林;它变得越来越重;他试图把它但它加强了痛苦,马尔福,嘲笑他他纠结,那么马尔福变成鹰钩鼻的老师,斯内普,他的笑变得高和冷——有一阵绿光和哈利醒来,出汗和颤抖。

He rolled over and fell asleep again, and when he woke next day, he didn’t remember the dream at all.

他翻了个身，又睡着了。第二天醒来时，他根本不记得做了什么梦。
