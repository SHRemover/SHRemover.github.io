Harry Potter and The Sorcerer’s Stone

Chapter Six The Journey from Platform Nine and Three-quarters

第六章 从九又四分之三站台开始的旅程

Harry’s last month with the Dursleys wasn’t fun. True, Dudley was now so scared of Harry he wouldn’t stay in the same room, while Aunt Petunia and Uncle Vernon didn’t shut Harry in his cupboard, force him to do anything, or shout at him — in fact, they didn’t speak to him at all. Half terrified, half furious, they acted as though any chair with Harry in it were empty. Although this was an improvement in many ways, it did become a bit depressing after a while.

哈利和德思礼一家在一起的最后一个月并不有趣。的确，达力现在非常害怕哈利，不愿和他呆在同一个房间里，而佩妮姨妈和维能姨丈也没有把哈利关在柜子里，没有强迫他做任何事，也没有对他大喊大叫——事实上，他们根本不和他说话。他们半害怕，半愤怒，表现得好像哈利坐的椅子都是空的。虽然这在很多方面是一个进步，但过了一段时间后，它确实变得有点令人沮丧。

Harry kept to his room, with his new owl for company. He had decided to call her Hedwig, a name he had found in A History of Magic. His school books were very interesting. He lay on his bed reading late into the night, Hedwig swooping in and out of the open window as she pleased. It was lucky that Aunt Petunia didn’t come in to vacuum anymore, because Hedwig kept bringing back dead mice. Every night before he went to sleep, Harry ticked off another day on the piece of paper he had pinned to the wall, counting down to September the first.

哈利呆在自己的房间里，和他的新猫头鹰做伴。他决定叫她海德薇，这是他在魔法史上找到的一个名字。他的课本很有趣。他躺在床上看书，一直读到深夜，海德薇高兴地从开着的窗户里飞进飞出。幸好佩妮姨妈不再来吸尘了，因为海德薇老是把死老鼠带回来。每天晚上睡觉前，哈利都会在钉在墙上的那张纸上再打上一天的记号，倒数到9月1日。

On the last day of August he thought he’d better speak to his aunt and uncle about getting to King’s Cross station the next day, so he went down to the living room where they were watching a quiz show on television. He cleared his throat to let them know he was there, and Dudley screamed and ran from the room.

八月的最后一天，他想他最好和他的叔叔婶婶谈谈第二天去国王十字车站的事，于是他来到客厅，他们正在看电视上的一个智力竞赛节目。他清了清嗓子，让他们知道他在那儿。达力尖叫着跑出了房间。

“Er — Uncle Vernon?”

“呃——弗农姨父?”

Uncle Vernon grunted to show he was listening.

弗农姨父哼了一声，表示他在听。

“Er — I need to be at King’s Cross tomorrow to — to go to Hogwarts.”

“呃——我明天得去国王十字车站——去霍格沃茨。”

Uncle Vernon grunted again.

弗农姨父又咕哝了一声。

“Would it be all right if you gave me a lift?”

“你可以载我一程吗?”

Grunt. Harry supposed that meant yes.

咕哝。哈利以为那是肯定的。

“Thank you.”

“谢谢你。”

He was about to go back upstairs when Uncle Vernon actually spoke.

他正要上楼去，维能姨丈居然开口了。

“Funny way to get to a wizards’ school, the train. Magic carpets all got punctures, have they?”

“去巫师学校的火车真有趣。”魔毯都扎破了，是吗?”

Harry didn’t say anything.

哈利什么也没说。

“Where is this school, anyway?”

“这所学校到底在哪儿?”

“I don’t know,” said Harry, realizing this for the first time. He pulled the ticket Hagrid had given him out of his pocket.

“我不知道。”哈利第一次意识到这一点。他从口袋里掏出海格给他的票。

“I just take the train from platform nine and three-quarters at eleven o’clock,” he read.

“我要在十一点整从九又四分之三站台上车,”他读道。

His aunt and uncle stared.

他的叔叔婶婶目瞪口呆。

“Platform what?”

“什么站台？”

“Nine and three-quarters.”

“九又四分之三。”

“Don’t talk rubbish,” said Uncle Vernon. “There is no platform nine and three-quarters.”

“别说废话，”弗农姨父说。“没有九又四分之三站台。”

“It’s on my ticket.”

“在我的票上。”

“Barking,” said Uncle Vernon, “howling mad, the lot of them. You’ll see. You just wait. All right, we’ll take you to King’s Cross. We’re going up to London tomorrow anyway, or I wouldn’t bother.”

“瞎说，”弗农姨父说，“你真是疯得够可以的了。明天你就知道了，走着瞧吧。行，我们带你去国王大道。要不是我们明天也要去伦敦，我才不会傻到自找麻烦呢。”

“Why are you going to London?” Harry asked, trying to keep things friendly.

“你为什么要去伦敦?”哈利问道，尽量保持友好。

“Taking Dudley to the hospital,” growled Uncle Vernon. “Got to have that ruddy tail removed before he goes to Smeltings.”

“送达力去医院。”弗农姨父咆哮道。“在他去斯梅廷之前，得把那红红的尾巴去掉。”

Harry woke at five o’clock the next morning and was too excited and nervous to go back to sleep. He got up and pulled on his jeans because he didn’t want to walk into the station in his wizard’s robes — he’d change on the train. He checked his Hogwarts list yet again to make sure he had everything he needed, saw that Hedwig was shut safely in her cage, and then paced the room, waiting for the Dursleys to get up. Two hours later, Harry’s huge, heavy trunk had been loaded into the Dursleys’ car, Aunt Petunia had talked Dudley into sitting next to Harry, and they had set off.

第二天早上5点钟，哈利醒了，他又兴奋又紧张，再也睡不着觉。他站起来，穿上牛仔裤，因为他不想穿着巫师袍走进车站——他会在火车上换的。他又检查了一遍霍格沃茨的名单，以确保他有所需要的一切，看到海德薇被安全地关在笼子里，然后在房间里踱来踱去，等着德思礼一家起床。两个小时后，哈利那又大又重的箱子被搬上了德思礼家的车，佩妮姨妈说服达德里坐在哈利旁边，他们就出发了。

They reached King’s Cross at half past ten. Uncle Vernon dumped Harry’s trunk onto a cart and wheeled it into the station for him. Harry thought this was strangely kind until Uncle Vernon stopped dead, facing the platforms with a nasty grin on his face.

他们十点半到达国王十字车站。弗农姨父把哈利的箱子扔到一辆手推车上，把它推到了车站。哈利觉得这是一种奇怪的仁慈，直到维能姨丈面露凶恶的笑容，面朝月台上停了下来。

“Well, there you are, boy. Platform nine — platform ten. Your platform should be somewhere in the middle, but they don’t seem to have built it yet, do they?”

“好了，傻小子，你到了.”九号站台——十号站台。你的平台应该在中间的某个地方，但他们似乎还没有建立它，是吗?

He was quite right, of course. There was a big plastic number nine over one platform and a big plastic number ten over the one next to it, and in the middle, nothing at all.

当然，他说得很对。在一个平台上有一个大大的塑料数字9，在它旁边的一个平台上有一个大大的塑料数字10，在中间，什么都没有。

“Have a good term,” said Uncle Vernon with an even nastier smile. He left without another word. Harry turned and saw the Dursleys drive away. All three of them were laughing. Harry’s mouth went rather dry. What on earth was he going to do? He was starting to attract a lot of funny looks, because of Hedwig. He’d have to ask someone.

“祝你有个好学期。”弗农姨父说着，脸上露出了更难听的笑容。他一句话也没说就走了。哈利转过身来，看见德思礼一家开车走了。他们三个都笑了。哈利的嘴变得相当干。他到底要做什么?因为海德薇，他开始吸引很多有趣的目光。他得找人问问。

He stopped a passing guard, but didn’t dare mention platform nine and three-quarters. The guard had never heard of Hogwarts and when Harry couldn’t even tell him what part of the country it was in, he started to get annoyed, as though Harry was being stupid on purpose. Getting desperate, Harry asked for the train that left at eleven o’clock, but the guard said there wasn’t one. In the end the guard strode away, muttering about time wasters. Harry was now trying hard not to panic. According to the large clock over the arrivals board, he had ten minutes left to get on the train to Hogwarts and he had no idea how to do it; he was stranded in the middle of a station with a trunk he could hardly lift, a pocket full of wizard money, and a large owl.

他拦住一个过路的卫兵，但不敢提起九又四分之三站台。警卫从来没有听说过霍格沃茨，当哈利甚至不能告诉他在这个国家的哪个地方时，他开始生气了，好像哈利是故意犯傻似的。哈利绝望地要了11点出发的火车，但列车员说没有。最后，卫兵大步走开了，嘴里嘟囔着浪费时间的话。哈利现在努力不让自己惊慌失措。根据接机板上的大钟，他还有十分钟的时间登上去霍格沃茨的火车，但他不知道该怎么做;他被困在一个车站的中央，有一只箱子他几乎提不动，口袋里装满了巫师的钱，还有一只大猫头鹰。

Hagrid must have forgotten to tell him something you had to do, like tapping the third brick on the left to get into Diagon Alley. He wondered if he should get out his wand and start tapping the ticket inspector’s stand between platforms nine and ten.

海格一定忘了告诉他你必须做的事情，比如敲左边第三块砖才能进入对角巷。他不知道自己是否应该拿出魔杖，开始敲打九号站台和十号站台之间检票员的柜台。

At that moment a group of people passed just behind him and he caught a few words of what they were saying.

正在这时，一群人从他身后经过，他听见他们说了几句话。

“— packed with Muggles, of course—”

“-当然是麻瓜了-”

Harry swung round. The speaker was a plump woman who was talking to four boys, all with flaming red hair. Each of them was pushing a trunk like Harry’s in front of him — and they had an owl.

哈利转弯了。说话的是一个胖胖的女人，她正在和四个红头发的男孩说话。他们每个人都在推着一个像哈利的箱子——他们还有一只猫头鹰。

Heart hammering, Harry pushed his cart after them. They stopped and so did he, just near enough to hear what they were saying.

哈利的心怦怦直跳，他推着车跟在他们后面。他们停了下来，他也停了下来，就在他们说话的地方。

“Now, what’s the platform number?” said the boys’ mother.

“现在，月台是几号?”男孩的妈妈说。

“You’re not old enough, Ginny, now be quiet. All right, Percy, you go first.”

“你还不够大，金妮，现在安静点。”好吧，珀西，你先走。”

What looked like the oldest boy marched toward platforms nine and ten. Harry watched, careful not to blink in case he missed it — but just as the boy reached the dividing barrier between the two platforms, a large crowd of tourists came swarming in front of him and by the time the last backpack had cleared away, the boy had vanished.

看起来最大的男孩朝九号和十号站台走去。哈利注视着，小心地不眨眼，以防错过——但就在男孩走到两个站台中间的隔板时，一大群游客涌到了他面前，当最后一个背包被拿走时，男孩已经不见了。

“Fred, you next,” the plump woman said.

“弗雷德，下一个轮到你，”胖女人说。

“I’m not Fred, I’m George,” said the boy. “Honestly, woman, you call yourself our mother? Can’t you tell I’m George?”

“Sorry, George, dear.”

“对不起,乔治,亲爱的。”

“Only joking, I am Fred,” said the boy, and off he went. His twin called after him to hurry up, and he must have done so, because a second later, he had gone — but how had he done it?

“开玩笑的，我是弗雷德。”男孩说着就走了。他的孪生兄弟在他身后叫他快点，他一定是这么做的，因为一秒钟后，他就走了——可他是怎么做到的呢?

Now the third brother was walking briskly toward the barrier he was almost there — and then, quite suddenly, he wasn’t anywhere.

现在，第三个哥哥正轻快地朝栅栏走去——他已经快到了——然后，突然，不见了。

There was nothing else for it.

没有别的办法了。

“Excuse me,” Harry said to the plump woman.

“对不起，”哈利对那个胖女人说。

“你好，亲爱的，”她说。“第一次来霍格沃茨?”罗恩也是新的。”

She pointed at the last and youngest of her sons. He was tall, thin, and gangling, with freckles, big hands and feet, and a long nose.

她指着她最小的儿子。他又高又瘦又瘦，长着雀斑，手脚很大，鼻子很长。

“Yes,” said Harry. “The thing is — the thing is, I don’t know how to—”

“是的,”哈利说。“问题是——问题是，我不知道该怎么做——”

“How to get onto the platform?” she said kindly, and Harry nodded.

“怎么到站台上去?”她亲切地说，哈利点了点头。

“Not to worry,” she said. “All you have to do is walk straight at the barrier between platforms nine and ten. Don’t stop and don’t be scared you’ll crash into it, that’s very important. Best do it at a bit of a run if you’re nervous. Go on, go now before Ron.”

“别担心，”她说。“你所要做的就是径直走到9号站台和10号站台之间的护栏处。不要停下来，不要害怕你会撞到它，这是非常重要的。如果你紧张的话，最好在跑步的时候做。走吧，走在罗恩前面。”

“Er — okay,” said Harry.

“呃——好吧，”哈利说。

He pushed his trolley around and stared at the barrier. It looked very solid.

他推着手推车，盯着栅栏看。它看起来很结实。

He started to walk toward it. People jostled him on their way to platforms nine and ten. Harry walked more quickly. He was going to smash right into that barrier and then he’d be in trouble — leaning forward on his cart, he broke into a heavy run — the barrier was coming nearer and nearer — he wouldn’t be able to stop — the cart was out of control — he was a foot away — he closed his eyes ready for the crash —

他开始朝它走去。在去九号站台和十号站台的路上，人们推搡着他。哈利走得更快了。正好他要打破屏障,然后他就麻烦了,在他的三轮车上身体向前倾斜,他闯进一个沉重的运行的障碍来越来越近——他无法阻止马车失控——他是在一步之遥——他闭上眼睛准备好崩溃

It didn’t come⋯ he kept on running⋯ he opened his eyes. A scarlet steam engine was waiting next to a platform packed with people. A sign overhead said Hogwarts’ Express, eleven o’clock. Harry looked behind him and saw a wrought-iron archway where the barrier had been, with the words Platform Nine and Three-Quarters on it, He had done it.

它没来⋯他继续运行⋯他睁开眼睛。一辆鲜红色的蒸汽机正等在一个挤满人的站台旁。头顶上有块牌子写着:“11点，霍格沃茨特快列车。”哈利回头一看，看见一道锻铁拱门，就在屏障所在的地方，上面写着“九又四分之三站台”。

Smoke from the engine drifted over the heads of the chattering crowd, while cats of every color wound here and there between their legs. Owls hooted to one another in a disgruntled sort of way over the babble and the scraping of heavy trunks.

引擎冒出的烟从叽叽喳喳的人群头上飘过，各种颜色的猫在两腿之间到处打伤。猫头鹰在叽叽喳喳声和沉重的树干摩擦声中不满地互相鸣叫。

The first few carriages were already packed with students, some hanging out of the window to talk to their families, some fighting over seats. Harry pushed his cart off down the platform in search of an empty seat. He passed a round-faced boy who was saying, “Gran, I’ve lost my toad again.”

最初的几节车厢里已经挤满了学生，有的挂在窗外和家人聊天，有的在争夺座位。哈利推着马车下了站台，想找个空位。他经过一个圆脸的男孩，男孩说:“奶奶，我又把癞蛤蟆弄丢了。”

“Oh, Neville,” he heard the old woman sigh.

“噢，纳威，”他听到老妇人叹了口气。

A boy with dreadlocks was surrounded by a small crowd.

一个留长发绺的男孩被一小群人包围了。

“Give us a look, Lee, go on.”

“给我们看看，李，继续。”

The boy lifted the lid of a box in his arms, and the people around him shrieked and yelled as something inside poked out a long, hairy leg.

男孩把一个盒子的盖子抱在怀里，盒子里有个东西伸出一条毛茸茸的长腿，周围的人尖叫着。

Harry pressed on through the crowd until he found an empty compartment near the end of the train. He put Hedwig inside first and then started to shove and heave his trunk toward the train door. He tried to lift it up the steps but could hardly raise one end and twice he dropped it painfully on his foot.

哈利从人群中挤了过去，直到他在火车的尾部发现了一个空车厢。他先把海德薇放了进去，然后开始用力把他的箱子推到车门。他试着把它抬上台阶，但一头几乎抬不起来，有两次他痛苦地把它摔在脚上。

“Want a hand?” It was one of the red-haired twins he’d followed through the barrier.

“要帮忙吗?那是他跟着穿过栅栏的一对红头发双胞胎中的一个。

“是的，请。”哈利气喘吁吁地说。

“Oy, Fred! C’mere and help!”

With the twins’ help, Harry’s trunk was at last tucked away in a corner of the compartment.

在双胞胎的帮助下，哈利的箱子终于被塞进了车厢的一个角落。

“Thanks,” said Harry, pushing his sweaty hair out of his eyes.

“谢谢。”哈利说着，把满头大汗的头发从眼睛里拨开。

“What’s that?” said one of the twins suddenly, pointing at Harry’s lightning scar.

“那是什么?双胞胎中的一个突然指着哈利闪电般的伤疤说。

“Blimey,” said the other twin. “Are you — ?”

“天哪，”另一个双胞胎说。“你是——?”

“He is,” said the first twin. “Aren’t you?” he added to Harry.

“是的，”第一个双胞胎说。“是吗?他又对哈利说。

“Harry Potter.” chorused the twins.

”哈利·波特。双胞胎异口同声地说。

“哦，是他。”“我是说，是的，我是。”

The two boys gawked at him, and Harry felt himself turning red. Then, to his relief, a voice came floating in through the train’s open door.

两个男孩呆呆地看着他，哈利觉得自己脸红了。然后，一个声音从火车敞开的门里飘了进来，使他松了一口气。

“Fred? George? Are you there?”

“弗雷德?乔治?你在那里么?”

“Coming, Mom.”

“来了,妈妈。”

With a last look at Harry, the twins hopped off the train.

双胞胎最后看了哈利一眼，跳下了火车。

Harry sat down next to the window where, half hidden, he could watch the red-haired family on the platform and hear what they were saying. Their mother had just taken out her handkerchief.

哈利在窗边坐了下来，半掩着身子，望着台上红头发的一家人，听着他们在说些什么。他们的母亲刚刚拿出手帕。

“Ron, you’ve got something on your nose.”

“罗恩，你鼻子上有东西。”

The youngest boy tried to jerk out of the way, but she grabbed him and began rubbing the end of his nose.

最小的男孩想把他拉开，但她抓住了他，开始揉他的鼻尖。

最大的男孩大步走了进来。他已经换上霍格沃茨的黑色长袍，哈利注意到胸前有一枚闪闪发光的银色徽章，上面写着字母P。

“呆不了多久，妈妈，”他说。“我在前面，班长们有两个包厢——”

“哦，你是班长吗，珀西?”双胞胎中的一个带着非常惊讶的神情说。“你应该说点什么，我们不知道。”

“等一下，我想我记得他说过这件事。”另一个双胞胎说。“说过一次——“

一条狭窄的小路突然通向一个黑色的大湖的边缘。在另一边的一座高山顶上，它的窗户在繁星点点的天空中闪闪发光，是一座有着许多塔楼和塔楼的巨大城堡。

“No more’n four to a boat!” Hagrid called, pointing to a fleet of little boats sitting in the water by the shore. Harry and Ron were followed into their boat by Neville and Hermione.

“一艘船上再也没有四个人了!”海格指着岸边的一队小船喊道。纳威和赫敏跟着哈利和罗恩上了船。

“Everyone in?” shouted Hagrid, who had a boat to himself. “Right then — FORWARD!”

“每个人都在吗?海格喊道，他自己有一条船。“就在那时——向前!”

And the fleet of little boats moved off all at once, gliding across the lake, which was as smooth as glass. Everyone was silent, staring up at the great castle overhead. It towered over them as they sailed nearer and nearer to the cliff on which it stood.

一队小船立刻出发了，滑过像玻璃一样光滑的湖面。每个人都沉默着，凝望着头顶上的城堡。当他们航行越来越接近它所在的悬崖时，它高耸入云。

“Heads down!” yelled Hagrid as the first boats reached the cliff; they all bent their heads and the little boats carried them through a curtain of ivy that hid a wide opening in the cliff face. They were carried along a dark tunnel, which seemed to be taking them right underneath the castle, until they reached a kind of underground harbor, where they clambered out onto rocks and pebbles.

“低头!当第一艘船到达悬崖时，海格喊道。他们都低下头来，小船载着他们穿过常春藤做成的帘子。他们被抬着沿着一条黑暗的隧道往前走，这条隧道似乎就在城堡下面，一直走到一个类似地下港口的地方，在那里他们爬上岩石和卵石。

“Oy, you there! Is this your toad?” said Hagrid, who was checking the boats as people climbed out of them.

“哦,你在那里!这是你的蟾蜍吗?海格说，人们从船里爬出来时，他正在检查船。

“Trevor!” cried Neville blissfully, holding out his hands. Then they clambered up a passageway in the rock after Hagrid’s lamp, coming out at last onto smooth, damp grass right in the shadow of the castle.

“特雷弗!纳威高兴地叫着，伸出双手。然后，他们跟在海格的灯后面，爬上岩石上的一条通道，最后走了出来，来到城堡阴影下光滑潮湿的草地上。

They walked up a flight of stone steps and crowded around the huge, oak front door.

他们走上一段石阶，挤在橡木大门的周围。

“Everyone here? You there, still got yer toad?”

“每个人都在这里吗?你在那儿，还有你的蟾蜍吗?”

Hagrid raised a gigantic fist and knocked three times on the castle door.

海格举起一个巨大的拳头，在城堡的门上敲了三下。
