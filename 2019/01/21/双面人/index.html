Harry Potter and The Sorcerer’s Stone

Chapter Seventeen The Man with Two Faces

第十七章 双面人

It was Quirrell.

是奇洛。

“You!” gasped Harry.

“你!”哈利喘着粗气。

Quirrell smiled. His face wasn’t twitching at all.

奇洛笑了。他的脸一点也不抽搐。

“Me,” he said calmly. “I wondered whether I’d be meeting you here, Potter.”

“我，”他平静地说。“我想知道我是否会在这里遇见你，波特。”

“But I thought — Snape—”

“可是我想——斯内普——”

“Severus?” Quirrell laughed, and it wasn’t his usual quivering treble, either, but cold and sharp. “Yes, Severus does seem the type, doesn’t he? So useful to have him swooping around like an overgrown bat. Next to him, who would suspect p-p-poor, st-stuttering P-Professor Quirrell?”

“斯内普?奇洛笑了，这也不是他平常那种颤颤巍巍的高音，而是冰冷而尖利的。“是的，斯内普似乎就是那种人，不是吗?”让他像一只长得太大的蝙蝠那样飞来飞去真是太有用了。在他旁边，谁会怀疑可怜的、口吃的教授奇洛呢?”

Harry couldn’t take it in. This couldn’t be true, it couldn’t.

哈利无法接受。这不可能是真的，不可能。

“But Snape tried to kill me!”

“可是斯内普想杀我!”

“No, no, no. I tried to kill you. Your friend Miss Granger accidentally knocked me over as she rushed to set fire to Snape at that Quidditch match. She broke my eye contact with you. Another few seconds and I’d have got you off that broom. I’d have managed it before then if Snape hadn’t been muttering a countercurse, trying to save you.”

“不,不,不。我想杀了你。你的朋友格兰杰小姐在魁地奇比赛中冲向斯内普放火的时候不小心把我撞倒了。她打断了我和你的眼神交流。再过几秒钟我就把你从扫帚上弄下来。要不是斯内普一直在咕哝着咒语，想救你，我早就想好了。

“Snape was trying to save me?”

“斯内普想救我?”

“Of course,” said Quirrell coolly. “Why do you think he wanted to referee your next match? He was trying to make sure I didn’t do it again. Funny, really⋯ he needn’t have bothered. I couldn’t do anything with Dumbledore watching. All the other teachers thought Snape was trying to stop Gryffindor from winning, he did make himself unpopular⋯ and what a waste of time, when after all that, I’m going to kill you tonight.”

“当然。”奇洛冷冷地说。“你认为他为什么要裁判你的下一场比赛?”他想确保我不会再犯同样的错误。有趣,真⋯他本不必烦恼。在邓布利多的注视下，我什么也做不了。所有其他的老师认为斯内普试图阻止格兰芬多赢了,他让自己不受欢迎⋯浪费时间,毕竟,今晚我要杀了你。”

Quirrell snapped his fingers. Ropes sprang out of thin air and wrapped themselves tightly around Harry.

奇洛打了个响指。绳子从天而落，紧紧地缠绕着哈利。

“You’re too nosy to live, Potter. Scurrying around the school on Halloween like that, for all I knew you’d seen me coming to look at what was guarding the Stone.”

“你太爱管闲事了，不能活下去，波特。”万圣节那天在学校里跑来跑去的，我就知道你看到我来这里是为了看守卫魔法石的东西。”

“You let the troll in?”

“你让巨魔进来了?”

“Certainly. I have a special gift with trolls — you must have seen what I did to the one in the chamber back there? Unfortunately, while everyone else was running around looking for it, Snape, who already suspected me, went straight to the third floor to head me off — and not only did my troll fail to beat you to death, that three-headed dog didn’t even manage to bite Snape’s leg off properly.

“当然。我对巨魔有特殊的天赋——你一定看到我对后面房间里的那个巨魔做了什么吧?不幸的是，当大家都在到处找它的时候，斯内普，这个早就怀疑我的人，径直跑到三楼来阻止我——不仅我的巨魔没能把你打死，那只三头狗甚至没能把斯内普的腿咬下来。

“Now, wait quietly, Potter. I need to examine this interesting mirror.”

“现在，静静地等着，波特。我需要检查这面有趣的镜子。”

It was only then that Harry realized what was standing behind Quirrell. It was the Mirror of Erised.

直到这时，哈利才意识到奇洛背后站着什么。那是厄里斯的镜子。

“This mirror is the key to finding the Stone,” Quirrell murmured, tapping his way around the frame. “Trust Dumbledore to come up with something like this⋯ but he’s in London⋯ I’ll be far away by the time he gets back⋯”

“这面镜子是找到魔法石的钥匙。”奇洛喃喃地说，一面用手在镜框上敲敲打打。”邓布利多信任想出这样的⋯⋯我会在伦敦但是他远离他回来的时候⋯”

All Harry could think of doing was to keep Quirrell talking and stop him from concentrating on the mirror.

哈利所能想到的就是让奇洛不停地说话，不让他把注意力集中在镜子上。

“I saw you and Snape in the forest — ” he blurted out.

“我看见你和斯内普在森林里——”他脱口而出。

“Yes,” said Quirrell idly, walking around the mirror to look at the back. “He was on to me by that time, trying to find out how far I’d got. He suspected me all along. Tried to frighten me — as though he could, when I had Lord Voldemort on my side⋯”

“是的，”奇洛懒洋洋地说，一面绕着镜子走过去看看后面。“那时他已经明白了我的意思，想知道我已经走了多远。”他一直怀疑我。想吓唬我,虽然他可以,当我在我身边⋯伏地魔”

Quirrell came back out from behind the mirror and stared hungrily into it.

奇洛从镜子后面走出来，饥渴地盯着镜子。

“I see the Stone⋯ I’m presenting it to my master⋯ but where is it?”

“我看到石头⋯我呈现我的主人⋯但是在哪里?”

Harry struggled against the ropes binding him, but they didn’t give. He had to keep Quirrell from giving his whole attention to the mirror.

哈利挣扎着挣脱绑着他的绳子，但他们没有松开。他得阻止奇洛把全部注意力都集中在镜子上。

“But Snape always seemed to hate me so much.”

“可是斯内普似乎总是那么恨我。”

“Oh, he does,” said Quirrell casually, “heavens, yes. He was at Hogwarts with your father, didn’t you know? They loathed each other. But he never wanted you dead.”

“噢，是的，”奇洛漫不经心地说，“天哪!他和你父亲在霍格沃茨，你不知道吗?他们彼此厌恶。但他从不想让你死。”

“But I heard you a few days ago, sobbing — I thought Snape was threatening you⋯”

“但是我听说你几天前,哭泣——我认为斯内普是威胁你⋯”

For the first time, a spasm of fear flitted across Quirrell’s face.

奇洛的脸上第一次感到一阵恐惧。

“Sometimes,” he said, “I find it hard to follow my master’s instructions — he is a great wizard and I am weak—”

“有时候，”他说，“我发现很难按照我主人的指示去做——他是一个伟大的巫师，而我很虚弱——”

“You mean he was there in the classroom with you?” Harry gasped.

“你是说他和你在教室里?””哈利喘着粗气。

“He is with me wherever I go,” said Quirrell quietly. “I met him when I traveled around the world. A foolish young man I was then, full of ridiculous ideas about good and evil. Lord Voldemort showed me how wrong I was. There is no good and evil, there is only power, and those too weak to seek it⋯ Since then, I have served him faithfully, although I have let him down many times. He has had to be very hard on me.” Quirrell shivered suddenly. “He does not forgive mistakes easily. When I failed to steal the stone from Gringotts, he was most displeased. He punished me⋯ decided he would have to keep a closer watch on me⋯”

“无论我走到哪里，他都和我在一起。”奇洛平静地说。我是在环游世界的时候认识他的。那时我是个愚蠢的年轻人，满脑子关于善恶的荒谬想法。伏地魔让我明白我错了。没有善恶,只有权力,和那些太弱寻求⋯从那时起,我有他忠实地,尽管我多次让他失望。他对我很严厉。奇洛突然打了个寒颤。他不轻易原谅错误。当我没能从古灵阁偷到那块石头时，他非常不高兴。他惩罚我⋯决定他会更为密切的关注我⋯”

Quirrell’s voice trailed away. Harry was remembering his trip to Diagon Alley — how could he have been so stupid? He’d seen Quirrell there that very day, shaken hands with him in the Leaky Cauldron.

奇洛的声音渐渐消失了。哈利想起了他去对角巷的那次旅行——他怎么会这么笨呢?那天他在破釜酒吧里看见奇洛跟他握手。

Quirrell cursed under his breath.

奇洛低声咒骂。

“I don’t understand⋯ is the Stone inside the mirror? Should I break it?”

“我不明白⋯镜子内的石头吗?我应该打破它吗?”

Harry’s mind was racing.

哈利的脑子在飞快地转。

What I want more than anything else in the world at the moment, he thought, is to find the Stone before Quirrell does. So if I look in the mirror, I should see myself finding it — which means I’ll see where it’s hidden! But how can I look without Quirrell realizing what I’m up to?

此刻，我最想做的事，他想，就是在奇洛找到那块石头之前找到它。所以如果我照镜子，我应该看到我自己找到了它——这意味着我将看到它藏在哪里!可是，我怎么能不让奇洛知道我在干什么呢?

He tried to edge to the left, to get in front of the glass without Quirrell noticing, but the ropes around his ankles were too tight: he tripped and fell over. Quirrell ignored him. He was still talking to himself.

他试着往左挪，想在奇洛不注意的情况下走到镜子前面，可是脚踝上的绳子太紧了，他绊了一跤。奇洛无视他。他还在自言自语。

“What does this mirror do? How does it work? Help me, Master!”

“这面镜子是干什么用的?”它是如何工作的?帮助我,主人!”

And to Harry’s horror, a voice answered, and the voice seemed to come from Quirrell himself.

令哈利惊恐的是，一个声音应声而出，那声音似乎是奇洛本人发出的。

“Use the boy⋯ Use the boy⋯”

”男孩使用男孩⋯⋯”

Quirrell rounded on Harry.

奇洛突然转向哈利。

“Yes — Potter — come here.”

“是的——波特——到这儿来。”

He clapped his hands once, and the ropes binding Harry fell off. Harry got slowly to his feet.

他拍了拍手，捆绑哈利的绳子掉了下来。哈利慢慢地站了起来。

“Come here,” Quirrell repeated. “Look in the mirror and tell me what you see.”

“过来，”奇洛重复道。“照照镜子，告诉我你看到了什么。”

Harry walked toward him.

哈利朝他走去。

I must lie, he thought desperately . I must look and lie about what I see, that’s all.

我必须撒谎，他绝望地想。我必须对我所看到的一切撒谎，仅此而已。

Quirrell moved close behind him. Harry breathed in the funny smell that seemed to come from Quirrell’s turban. He closed his eyes, stepped in front of the mirror, and opened them again.

奇洛紧跟在他后面。哈利闻到一股怪味，好像是从奇洛的头巾里飘出来的。他闭上眼睛，走到镜子前面，又睁开了眼睛。

He saw his reflection, pale and scared-looking at first. But a moment later, the reflection smiled at him. It put its hand into its pocket and pulled out a blood-red stone. It winked and put the Stone back in its pocket — and as it did so, Harry felt something heavy drop into his real pocket. Somehow — incredibly — he’d gotten the Stone.

他看到了自己的倒影，起初显得苍白而恐惧。但过了一会儿，镜子里的倒影朝他微笑。它把手伸进口袋，掏出一块血红色的石头。它眨眨眼，把魔法石放回口袋里——哈利感到有什么沉重的东西掉进了他真正的口袋。不知怎么的——难以置信的——他得到了那块石头。

“Well?” said Quirrell impatiently. “What do you see?”

“嗯?奇洛不耐烦地说。“你看到了什么?”

Harry screwed up his courage.

哈利鼓起勇气。

“I see myself shaking hands with Dumbledore,” he invented. “I — I’ve won the house cup for Gryffindor.”

“我看见自己在和邓布利多握手。”“我——我为格兰芬多赢得了学院杯。”

Quirrell cursed again.

奇洛被诅咒了。

“Get out of the way,” he said. As Harry moved aside, he felt the Sorcerer’s Stone against his leg. Dare he make a break for it?

“闪开，”他说。当哈利挪到一边时，他感到魔法石碰到了他的腿。他敢闯一闯吗?

But he hadn’t walked five paces before a high voice spoke, though Quirrell wasn’t moving his lips.

可是他还没走到五步，就有一个高亢的声音说话了，虽然奇洛的嘴唇一动也不动。

“He lies⋯ He lies⋯”

“他的谎言他⋯⋯”

“Potter, come back here!” Quirrell shouted. “Tell me the truth! What did you just see?”

“波特，回来!””奇洛喊道。“告诉我实话!”你刚才看到了什么?”

The high voice spoke again.

高嗓门又说话了。

“Let me speak to him⋯ face-to-face⋯”

“让我和他谈谈面对面⋯⋯”

“Master, you are not strong enough!”

“主人，你还不够强壮!”

“I have strength enough⋯ for this⋯”

“我有足够力量⋯⋯”

Harry felt as if Devil’s Snare was rooting him to the spot. He couldn’t move a muscle. Petrified, he watched as Quirrell reached up and began to unwrap his turban. What was going on? The turban fell away. Quirrell’s head looked strangely small without it. Then he turned slowly on the spot.

哈利觉得魔鬼的圈套好像要把他牢牢地套住。他一动也动不了。他呆住了，看着奇洛伸手打开他的头巾。发生了什么?头巾掉了下来。没有它，奇洛的头看上去小得出奇。然后他原地慢慢地转过身来。

Harry would have screamed, but he couldn’t make a sound. Where there should have been a back to Quirrell’s head, there was a face, the most terrible face Harry had ever seen. It was chalk white with glaring red eyes and slits for nostrils, like a snake.

哈利会尖叫，但他发不出声音。在奇洛的头后面本应该有一张脸的地方，却出现了一张脸，哈利从未见过的最可怕的脸。它是白垩白色的，有一双耀眼的红眼睛和鼻孔，像一条蛇。

“Harry Potter⋯ ” it whispered.

“哈利·波特⋯”小声说。

Harry tried to take a step backward but his legs wouldn’t move.

哈利试图后退一步，但他的腿一动也不能动。

“See what I have become?” the face said. “Mere shadow and vapor⋯ I have form only when I can share another’s body⋯ but there have always been those willing to let me into their hearts and minds⋯ Unicorn blood has strengthened me, these past weeks⋯ you saw faithful Quirrell drinking it for me in the forest⋯ and once I have the Elixir of Life, I will be able to create a body of my own⋯ Now⋯ why don’t you give me that Stone in your pocket?”

“看见我变成什么样子了吗?”那张脸说。“纯粹的阴影和蒸汽⋯我只有当我可以分享别人的身体⋯但一直都有那些愿意让我到他们的心灵和思想⋯独角兽的血液,都帮助了我过去几周⋯你看到在森林里对我忠实的奇洛喝⋯一旦我有长生不老药,我可以创建一个自己的身体现在⋯⋯你为什么不给我口袋里那块石头吗?”

So he knew. The feeling suddenly surged back into Harry’s legs. He stumbled backward.

所以他知道。这种感觉突然又涌回到哈利的腿上。他跌跌撞撞地向后倒去。

“Don’t be a fool,” snarled the face. “Better save your own life and join me⋯ or you’ll meet the same end as your parents⋯ They died begging me for mercy⋯”

“别傻了，”那张脸咆哮着说。“更好的节省你自己的生活和和我一起⋯或者你会遇到相同的结束你的父母慈爱⋯⋯他们死求我”

“LIAR!” Harry shouted suddenly.

“骗子!哈利突然喊道

Quirrell was walking backward at him, so that Voldemort could still see him. The evil face was now smiling.

奇洛朝他后退了几步，这样伏地魔还能看见他。那张邪恶的脸现在笑了。

“How touching⋯ ” it hissed. “I always value bravery⋯ Yes, boy, your parents were brave⋯ I killed your father first; and he put up a courageous fight⋯ but your mother needn’t have died⋯ she was trying to protect you⋯ Now give me the Stone, unless you want her to have died in vain.”

“如何触摸⋯”它嘶嘶地叫着。“我总是价值勇敢⋯是的,孩子,你的父母是勇敢⋯我杀了你的父亲;他拿出一个勇敢战斗⋯但你母亲不必她试图保护你现在⋯⋯去世给我石头,除非你想让她白白牺牲。”

“NEVER!”

“从来没有!”

Harry sprang toward the flame door, but Voldemort screamed “SEIZE HIM!” and the next second, Harry felt Quirrell’s hand close on his wrist. At once, a needle-sharp pain seared across Harry’s scar; his head felt as though it was about to split in two; he yelled, struggling with all his might, and to his surprise, Quirrell let go of him. The pain in his head lessened — he looked around wildly to see where Quirrell had gone, and saw him hunched in pain, looking at his fingers — they were blistering before his eyes.

哈利扑向火焰门，伏地魔尖叫道:“抓住他!”接着，哈利感到奇洛的手紧紧地抓住了他的手腕。哈利的伤疤立刻感到一阵刺痛;他的头仿佛要裂成两半;他拼命挣扎着喊道，奇洛竟然放开了他。他的头痛减轻了——他发疯似地环顾四周，想看看奇洛去了哪里，只见奇洛痛苦地弓着背，望着自己的手指——手指在他眼前起了水泡。

“Seize him! SEIZE HIM!” shrieked Voldemort again, and Quirrell lunged, knocking Harry clean off his feet landing on top of him, both hands around Harry’s neck — Harry’s scar was almost blinding him with pain, yet he could see Quirrell howling in agony.

“抓住他!抓住他!伏地魔又尖叫了一声，奇洛一跃而起，把哈利撞倒在地，双手搂住了哈利的脖子。哈利的伤疤痛得几乎要把他弄瞎了。

“Master, I cannot hold him — my hands — my hands!”

“主人，我抓不住他——我的手——我的手!”

And Quirrell, though pinning Harry to the ground with his knees, let go of his neck and stared, bewildered, at his own palms — Harry could see they looked burned, raw, red, and shiny.

奇洛虽然用膝盖把哈利按倒在地，但还是放开了他的脖子，迷惑不解地盯着自己的手掌——哈利看得出来，他的手掌像是烧焦了的、生的、红的、发亮的。

“Then kill him, fool, and be done!” screeched Voldemort.

“那就杀了他吧，傻瓜，完事了!””伏地魔发出尖锐刺耳的声音。

Quirrell raised his hand to perform a deadly curse, but Harry, by instinct, reached up and grabbed Quirrell’s face —

奇洛举起手来要下一个致命的咒语，可是哈利本能地伸手抓住了他的脸

“AAAARGH!”

“啊。。。 !”

Quirrell rolled off him, his face blistering, too, and then Harry knew: Quirrell couldn’t touch his bare skin, not without suffering terrible pain — his only chance was to keep hold of Quirrell, keep him in enough pain to stop him from doing a curse.

奇洛从他身上滚了下来，脸上也起了水泡。这时哈利才明白:奇洛光着身子是碰不到皮肤的，而且还会感到剧痛——他唯一的机会就是抓住奇洛，让他忍受足够的痛苦，免得他诅咒别人。

Harry jumped to his feet, caught Quirrell by the arm, and hung on as tight as he could. Quirrell screamed and tried to throw Harry off — the pain in Harry’s head was building — he couldn’t see — he could only hear Quirrell’s terrible shrieks and Voldemort’s yells of, “KILL HIM! KILL HIM!” and other voices, maybe in Harry’s own head, crying, “Harry! Harry!”

哈利一跃而起，抓住奇洛的胳膊，紧紧地抓住不放。奇洛尖叫着试图把哈利甩掉——哈利的头越来越痛——他什么也看不见——他只能听到奇洛可怕的尖叫声和伏地魔“杀了他!”杀了他!还有其他的声音，也许是在哈利的脑海里，喊着:“哈利!”哈利!”

He felt Quirrell’s arm wrenched from his grasp, knew all was lost, and fell into blackness, down⋯ down⋯ down⋯

他觉得奇洛的手臂扭从他的掌握,知道一切都失去了,掉进了黑暗,下来⋯⋯⋯下来

Something gold was glinting just above him. The Snitch! He tried to catch it, but his arms were too heavy.

他头顶上有个金光闪闪的东西。告密者!他想抓住它，但是他的胳膊太重了。

He blinked. It wasn’t the Snitch at all. It was a pair of glasses. How strange.

他眨了眨眼睛。根本不是告密者。那是一副眼镜。多么奇怪。

He blinked again. The smiling face of Albus Dumbledore swam into view above him.

他又眨了眨眼睛。阿不思·邓布利多的笑脸浮现在他的头顶。

“Good afternoon, Harry,” said Dumbledore.

“下午好，哈利。”邓布利多说。

Harry stared at him. Then he remembered: “Sir! The Stone! It was Quirrell! He’s got the Stone! Sir, quick—”

哈利盯着他。然后他想起:“先生!石头!这是奇洛!他拿到石头了!先生,快- - - - - -”

“It’s not a stoat sandwich, is it?” said Harry anxiously, and at last Hagrid gave a weak chuckle.

“这不是白鼬三明治，对吗?”哈利焦急地说，最后海格发出了微弱的笑声。

“Nah. Dumbledore gave me the day off yesterday ter fix it. ‘course, he shoulda sacked me instead — anyway, got yeh this⋯”

“不。邓布利多昨天请了我一天假来修。”当然,他应该解雇我,不管怎样,有叶这⋯”

It seemed to be a handsome, leather-covered book. Harry opened it curiously. It was full of wizard photographs. Smiling and waving at him from every page were his mother and father.

那似乎是一本漂亮的皮面书。哈利好奇地打开它。里面全是巫师的照片。他的父母在每一页纸上向他微笑挥手。

“Sent owls off ter all yer parents’ old school friends, askin’ fer photos⋯ knew yeh didn’ have any⋯ d’yeh like it?”

“把猫头鹰ter所有父母的老校友,阿斯顿的带照片⋯知道叶⋯d ‘yeh喜欢它吗?”

Harry couldn’t speak, but Hagrid understood.

哈利说不出话来，但海格听得懂。

Harry made his way down to the end-of-year feast alone that night. He had been held up by Madam Pomfrey’s fussing about, insisting on giving him one last checkup, so the Great Hall was already full. It was decked out in the Slytherin colors of green and silver to celebrate Slytherin’s winning the house cup for the seventh year in a row. A huge banner showing the Slytherin serpent covered the wall behind the High Table.

那天晚上，哈利独自一人去参加年终宴会。他被庞弗雷夫人的忙乱耽搁了，她坚持要给他做最后一次检查，所以大厅里已经坐满了人。它被装饰成斯莱特林的绿色和银色，以庆祝斯莱特林连续七年赢得学院杯。高高的桌子后面的墙上挂着一面巨大的旗帜，上面写着斯莱特林蛇。

When Harry walked in there was a sudden hush, and then everybody started talking loudly at once. He slipped into a seat between Ron and Hermione at the Gryffindor table and tried to ignore the fact that people were standing up to look at him.

哈利走进去时，突然安静下来，然后大家立刻开始大声说话。在格兰芬多的餐桌上，他溜到罗恩和赫敏之间的一个座位上，试图无视人们都站起来看他的事实。

Fortunately, Dumbledore arrived moments later. The babble died away.

幸运的是，邓布利多过了一会儿就到了。胡言乱语逐渐消失了。

“Another year gone!” Dumbledore said cheerfully. “And I must trouble you with an old man’s wheezing waffle before we sink our teeth into our delicious feast. What a year it has been! Hopefully your heads are all a little fuller than they were⋯ you have the whole summer ahead to get them nice and empty before next year starts⋯

“一年不见了!邓布利多高兴地说。“在我们尽情享用美味佳肴之前，我得麻烦你吃一个老头子气喘吁吁的华夫饼。”这是多么好的一年啊!希望你的头都有点丰满比⋯你整个夏天提前让他们不错,明年开始之前空⋯

“Now, as I understand it, the house cup here needs awarding, and the points stand thus: In fourth place, Gryffindor, with three hundred and twelve points; in third, Hufflepuff, with three hundred and fifty-two; Ravenclaw has four hundred and twenty-six and Slytherin, four hundred and seventy-two.”

“现在，据我所知，这里需要颁发学院杯，分数是这样的:第四名格兰芬多，312分;第三名是赫奇帕奇学院，有352分;拉文克劳有426分，斯莱特林有472分。

A storm of cheering and stamping broke out from the Slytherin table. Harry could see Draco Malfoy banging his goblet on the table. It was a sickening sight.

斯莱特林的桌子上爆发出一阵欢呼声和跺脚声。哈利可以看见德拉科·马尔福把酒杯重重地摔在桌子上。这是令人作呕的景象。

“Yes, Yes, well done, Slytherin,” said Dumbledore. “However, recent events must be taken into account.”

“是的，是的，干得好，斯莱特林。”“然而，必须考虑到最近发生的事件。”

The room went very still. The Slytherins’ smiles faded a little.

房间里寂静无声。斯莱特林的笑容渐渐消失了。

“Ahem,” said Dumbledore. “I have a few last-minute points to dish out. Let me see. Yes⋯

“嗯,”邓布利多说。“我在最后一刻还有几点要说。让我看看。是的⋯

“First — to Mr. Ronald Weasley⋯”

“第一,罗纳德·韦斯莱先生⋯”

Ron went purple in the face; he looked like a radish with a bad sunburn.

罗恩的脸涨得通红;他看上去像被严重晒伤的萝卜。

“⋯ for the best-played game of chess Hogwarts has seen in many years, I award Gryffindor house fifty points.”

“最佳游戏国际象棋的游戏⋯霍格沃茨已经在许多年,我奖励格兰芬多50分。”

Gryffindor cheers nearly raised the bewitched ceiling; the stars overhead seemed to quiver. Percy could be heard telling the other prefects, “My brother, you know! My youngest brother! Got past McGonagall’s giant chess set!”

格兰芬多的欢呼声几乎把被魔咒笼罩的天花板都掀了起来;头顶上的星星似乎在颤动。可以听到珀西对其他级长说:“我的兄弟，你知道!我最小的弟弟!通过了麦格教授的大象棋组!”

At last there was silence again.

最后又沉默了。

“Second — to Miss Hermione Granger⋯ for the use of cool logic in the face of fire, I award Gryffindor house fifty points.”

“第二,赫敏·格兰杰小姐⋯使用很酷的逻辑在面对火灾,我奖格兰芬多50分。”

Hermione buried her face in her arms; Harry strongly suspected she had burst into tears. Gryffindors up and down the table were beside themselves — they were a hundred points up.

赫敏把脸埋在怀里;哈利强烈怀疑她哭了。格兰芬多的学生在桌子上爬上爬下都快疯了——他们的分数高出了100分。

“Third — to Mr. Harry Potter⋯ ” said Dumbledore. The room went deadly quiet. “⋯ for pure nerve and outstanding courage, I award Gryffindor house sixty points.”

“第三,哈利·波特先生⋯”邓布利多说。房间里静得要命。“⋯纯粹的神经和杰出的勇气,我奖励格兰芬多60点。”

The din was deafening. Those who could add up while yelling themselves hoarse knew that Gryffindor now had four hundred and seventy-two points — exactly the same as Slytherin. They had tied for the house cup — if only Dumbledore had given Harry just one more point.

喧闹声震耳欲聋。那些能把自己的声音喊得嘶哑的学生知道，格兰芬多现在得了472分——和斯莱特林完全一样。他们在学院杯上打成了平手——要是邓布利多再给哈利一分就好了。

Dumbledore raised his hand. The room gradually fell silent.

邓布利多举起了手。房间里渐渐安静下来。

“There are all kinds of courage,” said Dumbledore, smiling. “It takes a great deal of bravery to stand up to our enemies, but just as much to stand up to our friends. I therefore award ten points to Mr. Neville Longbottom.”

“有各种各样的勇气。”邓布利多微笑着说。“勇敢地面对敌人需要极大的勇气，但同样也需要勇敢地面对朋友。”因此，我给纳威·隆巴顿先生10分。”

Someone standing outside the Great Hall might well have thought some sort of explosion had taken place, so loud was the noise that erupted from the Gryffindor table. Harry, Ron, and Hermione stood up to yell and cheer as Neville, white with shock, disappeared under a pile of people hugging him. He had never won so much as a point for Gryffindor before. Harry, still cheering, nudged Ron in the ribs and pointed at Malfoy, who couldn’t have looked more stunned and horrified if he’d just had the Body-Bind Curse put on him.

站在大厅外面的人很可能以为发生了什么爆炸，格兰芬多餐桌上爆发出的声音太大了。哈利、罗恩和赫敏站起来欢呼，纳威吓得脸色发白，消失在一群拥抱他的人下面。他以前从来没有为格兰芬多赢过一分。哈利仍在欢呼，用胳膊肘捅了捅罗恩的肋骨，然后指着马尔福。如果马尔福刚刚被绑在身上的魔咒击中的话，他会显得无比震惊和恐惧。

“Which means,” Dumbledore called over the storm of applause, for even Ravenclaw and Hufflepuff were celebrating the downfall of Slytherin, “we need a little change of decoration.”

“这意味着，”邓布利多在雷文克劳和赫奇帕奇都在庆祝斯莱特林的垮台时，他的声音盖过了雷鸣般的掌声，“我们需要做一点小小的装饰。”

He clapped his hands. In an instant, the green hangings became scarlet and the silver became gold; the huge Slytherin serpent vanished and a towering Gryffindor lion took its place. Snape was shaking Professor McGonagall’s hand, with a horrible, forced smile. He caught Harry’s eye and Harry knew at once that Snape’s feelings toward him hadn’t changed one jot. This didn’t worry Harry. It seemed as though life would be back to normal next year, or as normal as it ever was at Hogwarts.

他拍着手。转眼之间，绿色的帷子变成了红色，银色的变成了金色;巨大的斯莱特林蛇消失了，取而代之的是一只高大的格兰芬多狮。斯内普和麦格教授握手，脸上带着可怕的、勉强的微笑。他引起了哈利的注意，哈利立刻明白斯内普对他的感情丝毫没有改变。哈利并不担心。似乎明年的生活就会恢复正常，或者和霍格沃茨以前一样。

It was the best evening of Harry’s life, better than winning at Quidditch, or Christmas, or knocking out mountain trolls⋯ he would never, ever forget tonight.

这是最好的晚上,哈利的生命,比赢得了魁地奇,或圣诞节,或者敲山巨魔⋯他永远不会忘记今晚。

Harry had almost forgotten that the exam results were still to come, but come they did. To their great surprise, both he and Ron passed with good marks; Hermione, of course, had the best grades of the first years. Even Neville scraped through, his good Herbology mark making up for his abysmal Potions one. They had hoped that Goyle, who was almost as stupid as he was mean, might be thrown out, but he had passed, too. It was a shame, but as Ron said, you couldn’t have everything in life.

哈利几乎忘记了考试成绩还没出来，可他们来了。令他们大为吃惊的是，他和罗恩都以优异的成绩通过了考试;当然，赫敏在第一年的成绩是最好的。甚至纳威也勉强通过了考试，他在草药课上的好成绩弥补了他糟糕透顶的魔药课。他们本来希望高尔这个几乎和他一样愚蠢和卑鄙的家伙会被赶出去，但他也通过了。真遗憾，但就像罗恩说的，你不可能拥有生活中的一切。

And suddenly, their wardrobes were empty, their trunks were packed, Neville’s toad was found lurking in a corner of the toilets; notes were handed out to all students, warning them not to use magic over the holidays (“I always hope they’ll forget to give us these,” said Fred Weasley sadly); Hagrid was there to take them down to the fleet of boats that sailed across the lake; they were boarding the Hogwarts Express; talking and laughing as the countryside became greener and tidier; eating Bettie Bott’s Every Flavor Beans as they sped past Muggle towns; pulling off their wizard robes and putting on jackets and coats; pulling into platform nine and three-quarters at King’s Cross Station.

突然间，他们的衣柜空了，箱子装得满满的，人们发现纳威的癞蛤蟆藏在厕所的一个角落里。所有的学生都发了纸条，警告他们不要在节日期间使用魔法(“我总是希望他们会忘记给我们这些，”弗雷德·韦斯莱悲伤地说);海格是来接他们的，带他们到湖对面的船队那里去。他们正在登上霍格沃茨特快列车;随着乡村变得更绿更整洁，人们有说有笑;吃着比比多味豆，他们飞快地经过麻瓜镇;脱下巫师袍，穿上夹克和外套;在国王十字车站进九又四分之三站台。

It took quite a while for them all to get off the platform. A wizened old guard was up by the ticket barrier, letting them go through the gate in twos and threes so they didn’t attract attention by all bursting out of a solid wall at once and alarming the Muggles.

他们花了好长时间才下了台。一个干瘪的老警卫站在检票口，让他们三三两两地穿过大门，免得他们突然从一堵坚实的墙里冲出来，吓着麻瓜，引起注意。

“You must come and stay this summer,” said Ron, “both of you — I’ll send you an owl.”

“你们今年夏天一定要来住，”罗恩说，“你们两个——我送你们一只猫头鹰。”

“Thanks,” said Harry, “I’ll need something to look forward to.” People jostled them as they moved forward toward the gateway back to the Muggle world. Some of them called:

“谢谢，”哈利说，“我需要一些期待的东西。”当他们向返回麻瓜世界的大门走去时，人们推搡着他们。其中一些人说:

“Bye, Harry!”

“再见,哈利!”

“See you, Potter!”

“见到你,波特!”

“Still famous,” said Ron, grinning at him.

“还是很有名。”罗恩说，朝他咧嘴一笑。

“Not where I’m going, I promise you,” said Harry.

“我不会去的，我向你保证。”

He, Ron, and Hermione passed through the gateway together. “There he is, Mom, there he is, look!”

他、罗恩和赫敏一起穿过大门。“他在那儿，妈妈，他在那儿，看!”

It was Ginny Weasley, Ron’s younger sister, but she wasn’t pointing at Ron.

那是金妮·韦斯莱，罗恩的妹妹，但她没有指着罗恩。

“Harry Potter!” she squealed. “Look, Mom! I can see—”

“哈利·波特!”她叫苦不迭。“看,妈妈!我可以看到,“

“Be quiet, Ginny, and it’s rude to point.”

“安静点，金妮，指指点点是不礼貌的。”

Mrs. Weasley smiled down at them.

韦斯莱夫人朝他们笑了笑。

“Busy year?” she said.

“忙碌的一年?”她说。

“Very,” said Harry. “Thanks for the fudge and the sweater, Mrs. Weasley.”

“非常,”哈利说。“谢谢你的软糖和毛衣，韦斯莱夫人。”

“Oh, it was nothing, dear.”

“哦，没什么，亲爱的。”

“Ready, are you?”

“准备好了,你呢?”

It was Uncle Vernon, still purple-faced, still mustached, still looking furious at the nerve of Harry, carrying an owl in a cage in a station full of ordinary people. Behind him stood Aunt Petunia and Dudley, looking terrified at the very sight of Harry.

那是弗农姨父，他的脸还是紫的，胡子还留着，对着哈利的神经还在发怒，在一个挤满了普通人的车站里，他把一只猫头鹰关在笼子里。他身后站着帕尤妮亚姨妈和达力，他们一看到哈利就惊恐万分。

“You must be Harry’s family!” said Mrs. Weasley.

“你一定是哈利的家人!”韦斯莱夫人说。

“In a manner of speaking,” said Uncle Vernon. “Hurry up, boy, we haven’t got all day.” He walked away.

“可以这么说，”维能姨丈说。“快点，孩子，我们没时间了。”他走开了。

Harry hung back for a last word with Ron and Hermione.

哈利犹豫了一下，想跟罗恩和赫敏说最后一句话。

“See you over the summer, then.”

“那么，夏天再见了。”

“Hope you have — er — a good holiday,” said Hermione, looking uncertainly after Uncle Vernon, shocked that anyone could be so unpleasant.

“希望你——呃——假期过得愉快。”赫敏疑惑地望着弗农姨父，惊讶地发现居然有人这么不愉快。

“Oh, I will,” said Harry, and they were surprised at the grin that was spreading over his face. “They don’t know we’re not allowed to use magic at home. I’m going to have a lot of fun with Dudley this summer⋯
